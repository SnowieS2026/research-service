## Detailed Findings

### 1. Open Redirect on app.grammarly.com (LOW)

**Endpoint:** https://app.grammarly.com/api/profile
**Method:** GET with ?next=https://evil.com

**Evidence:**
`
HTTP/1.1 301 Moved Permanently
Location: https://www.grammarly.com/1?next=https%3A%2F%2Fevil.com
Set-Cookie: redirect_location=eyJ0eXBlIjoiIiwibG9jYXRpb24iOiJodHRwczovL3d3dy5ncmFtbWFybHkuY29tLzE/bmV4dD1odHRwcyUzQSUyR
iUyRmV2aWwuY29tIn0=...
`

**Impact:** Phishing. The 
ext query parameter is reflected in a Location header to an external domain. The redirect_location cookie also encodes the destination (base64).

**Note:** This is a known pattern for Grammarly's auth flow but the 
ext parameter being user-controlled and reflected in a 301 to an external domain could be abused in phishing campaigns.
