## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Mexico–US Relations — Immigration and Tariffs (March 2026)
- **Mexico under pressure from Trump administration** over immigration and fentanyl trafficking.
- Trump threatening **25% tariffs on Mexican goods** if Mexico doesn't reduce the flow of migrants and drugs to the US.
- **President Sheinbaum responding with her own measures** — deploying the National Guard to the US border to intercept migrants.
- Mexico in a difficult position — Trump's tariffs would devastate the Mexican economy (82.7% of exports go to the US).
- **Mexico providing humanitarian visas** to Chinese and other migrants crossing Mexico to reach the US — a diplomatic gesture to Beijing.

### Mexico's Role in US Fentanyl Crisis (March 2026)
- **US accusing Mexican cartels and Chinese chemical suppliers** of fuelling the American fentanyl crisis.
- Trump demanding Mexico crack down on cartel finances and drug labs.
- Mexico's military deploying against cartels in Sinaloa and other regions — but the cartels remain powerful.

## 🇺🇸 USMCA — The Trade Agreement Under Stress (March 2026)

### USMCA at Risk — Trump's Tariff Threats (March 2026)
- **USMCA** (United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement) — the trade deal that underpins North American commerce — is under severe strain.
- Trump threatening to withdraw from USMCA or impose tariffs — all designed to bring manufacturing back to the US.
- **Mexico positioning itself as a "nearshoring" destination** — attracting Chinese and other Asian companies that want to access the US market without the political risk of manufacturing in China.
- **Tesla, Apple, and semiconductor firms** investing in Mexico — creating a high-tech manufacturing corridor near the US border.

## 🇲🇽 Domestic Politics — Sheinbaum's First Months (March 2026)

### President Claudia Sheinbaum — Scientist Leading Mexico (March 2026)
- **Claudia Sheinbaum** — Mexico's first female president, a climate scientist by training — governing since October 2024.
- Her MORENA party continuing to dominate Mexican politics — consolidating power after decades of PRI-PAN-PRD alternation.
- Sheinbaum's approach: **tactical pragmatism** — working with US despite nationalist rhetoric, maintaining cartel pressure, deepening economic ties with China.
- **Political opposition weak** — the old PRI/PAN parties fragmented and unable to challenge MORENA's dominance.

## 🛡️ Security — The Cartel War Continues (March 2026)

### Sinaloa Cartel Splintering — Violence Escalating (March 2026)
- The arrest of **El Chapo's sons** has caused the Sinaloa Cartel to splinter into violent factions — killing sprees in Sinaloa state.
- Mexico deploying the military and National Guard against cartel violence — but **400,000+ deaths** from cartel-related violence since the "War on Drugs" began in 2006.
- **Mexico's homicide rate** remains among the highest in the world — though declining slightly.
- US offering **bounties for cartel leaders** — deepening the entanglement of US and Mexican security.

## 🛢️ Energy — Mexico's Oil and Nearshoring

### Mexico's Oil Sector — Pemex Struggling (March 2026)
- **Pemex** (Mexico's state oil company) — one of the most indebted oil companies in the world.
- Mexico reversing the energy reforms of previous administrations — bringing oil and electricity back under state control.
- This is deterring some foreign investment — though nearshoring in manufacturing is more attractive.

### Nearshoring Boom — Mexico's Economic Opportunity (March 2026)
- **Nearshoring** from China to Mexico accelerating — driven by Trump's tariffs on Chinese goods.
- Mexico benefiting from US-China trade tensions — Chinese manufacturers setting up in Mexico to access the US market tariff-free.
- This is generating **manufacturing jobs** across northern Mexico — but also putting pressure on water and electricity infrastructure.

## 🌍 Foreign Relations — China and Latin America

### Mexico's Relationship with China — Trade but No Diplomacy (March 2026)
- Mexico is **China's largest Latin American trading partner** — bilateral trade exceeding $100B.
- Mexico does NOT have a free trade agreement with China — politically sensitive given USMCA obligations.
- Mexico has remained firmly in the US camp diplomatically — voting with the US at the UN more than any other Latin American country.
