## 7. Historical Context

### Timeline of Key Events

**Pre-17th Century:**
- Indigenous Austronesian peoples (no unified state)
- Brief Dutch and Spanish colonial presence (1624-1662)

**Qing Dynasty (1683-1895):**
- Annexed by Qing China
- Part of Fujian province, later made a province (1885)
- Limited Chinese settlement initially

**Japanese Colonial Period (1895-1945):**
- Ceded to Japan after First Sino-Japanese War (Treaty of Shimonoseki)
- Modernization: railways, education, industrialization
- Taiwanese fought for Japan in WWII
- Industrial base established by Japan

**Republic of China Era Begins (1945):**
- Japan surrenders Taiwan to ROC after WWII
- February 28 Incident (1947): Government massacre of civilians
- Martial law imposed (1947)

**KMT Retreat (1949):**
- Chinese Civil War ends with Communist victory
- ~2 million KMT soldiers and civilians flee to Taiwan
- ROC government relocates to Taipei
- Intent to "retake the mainland" declared

**Martial Law Era (1949-1987):**
- One-party KMT rule under Chiang Kai-shek
- White Terror: Political repression, thousands executed
- Economic miracle: Export-led growth begins
- Taiwan Miracle transforms economy

**Democratization (1987-2000):**
- Martial law lifted 1987
- First opposition party (DPP) legalized
- 1996: First direct presidential election (Lee Teng-hui wins)
- 2000: First peaceful transfer of power (DPP's Chen Shui-bian)

**Modern Democratic Era:**
- **2008-2016:** KMT President Ma Ying-jeou improves cross-strait relations
- **2016-2024:** DPP President Tsai Ing-wen asserts Taiwanese identity
- **2019:** Hong Kong protests increase Taiwan's rejection of "one country two systems"
- **2020:** Tsai re-elected with historic high votes
- **2022-Present:** Fourth Taiwan Strait Crisis begins after Pelosi visit
- **2024:** Lai Ching-te elected president, DPP loses legislative majority

### Historical Turning Points
1. **1895:** Japanese colonization begins
