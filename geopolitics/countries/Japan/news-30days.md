## 🇨🇳 China Relations — Strategic Rivalry Intensifies

### Japan's Response to Chinese Military Activity (March 2026)
- Japan scrambling fighter jets almost daily against Chinese aircraft near its airspace — the **Air Self-Defense Force (ASDF) operating at maximum tempo**.
- **Chinese coast guard vessels** regularly operating near the **Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands** — Japan-controlled but claimed by China.
- Japan strengthening its **anti-access/area-denial (A2/AD) capabilities** — acquiring advanced missiles, F-35s, and building up its Navy.

### Japan's Chip Industry — TSMC and Semiconductor Strategy (March 2026)
- **TSMC's Kumamoto plant (Kumamoto 1) began operations in 2024** — the world's most advanced semiconductor factory in Japan.
- Japan offering massive subsidies to attract chip manufacturing — **Rapidus** (Japanese consortium) targeting 2nm chip production by 2027.
- This is explicitly a ** China-proofing strategy** — reducing dependence on Taiwanese chips that could be disrupted by a Taiwan conflict.
- **Tokyo Electron** (3rd largest chip equipment maker) and other Japanese firms are critical nodes in the global semiconductor supply chain.

## 🇰🇵 North Korea — Missile Tests and Threat

### Kim Jong-un's Latest Provocations (March 2026)
- **North Korea tested another ballistic missile** — the latest in a series of tests that have included ICBMs capable of reaching the US mainland.
- Japan consistently in the range of North Korean missiles — the threat is immediate and existential for Japan.
- **Japan, US, and South Korea** conducting regular trilateral missile defence exercises — the threat is driving deeper security cooperation.

### Abductees — Japan's Emotional Priority (March 2026)
- Japan continuing to push North Korea on the **abductee issue** — Japanese citizens kidnapped by North Korean agents in the 1970s-80s.
- This remains an emotionally charged issue in Japan — and prevents any normalisation of relations with North Korea without resolution.

## 🛡️ Defence Modernisation — Breaking Post-War Constraints

### Japan's Defence Spending — From 1% to 2% GDP (March 2026)
- Japan committed to reaching **2% of GDP on defence** — a dramatic shift from the post-war 1% ceiling.
- **Counterstrike capabilities** — Japan developing the ability to strike enemy bases (contradicting its exclusively defensive post-war constitution).
- Japan acquiring **Tomahawk cruise missiles** from the US — capable of striking targets 1,000+ km away.
- **AEGIS Ashore** missile defence system — Japan deploying advanced radar and interceptors.

## 🏭 Economy

- **Japan's economy is the world's 4th largest** by nominal GDP — but growth is sluggish.
- **Deflation** remains a chronic problem — consumer prices and wages stagnant for decades.
- **Yen weakness** — the yen has weakened significantly, making Japanese assets cheaper for foreign buyers.
- **Automotive crisis** — Toyota, Honda, Nissan facing intense competition from Chinese EVs; Japan's automotive dominance eroding.
