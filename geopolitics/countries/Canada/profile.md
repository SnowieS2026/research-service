**Geography Summary:** Canada spans the northern half of North America, from the Atlantic to the Pacific and northward into the Arctic Ocean. The country is characterized by vast wilderness, the Rocky Mountains, boreal forests covering nearly 40% of its landmass, and the longest coastline in the world (202,080 km). The Arctic Archipelago comprises over 36,000 islands, increasingly strategic as Arctic ice recedes.

### Key Bilateral Relationships

**United States — The Indispensable Relationship**
- **Trade:** $1 trillion+ in annual bilateral trade; US is Canada's largest trading partner (75% of exports)
- **Defense:** NORAD (North American Aerospace Defense Command) — joint US-Canada command since 1958
- **Border:** World's longest undefended border (8,891 km); ~400,000 daily border crossings
- **USMCA/CUSMA:** Renegotiated NAFTA in force since 2020
- **Energy:** Canada is the largest foreign supplier of oil to the US (~4 million barrels/day)
- **Issues:** Trade disputes (softwood lumber, dairy), Buy American provisions, border security, Keystone XL cancellation

**United Kingdom — Historical Ties**
- Constitutional monarchy shared; King Charles III is Head of State
- Commonwealth member with deep historical, cultural, legal ties
- Post-Brexit trade: Canada-UK Trade Continuity Agreement (2021)
- Intelligence and defense cooperation through Five Eyes

**China — Complex Strategic Competition**
- **Trade:** China is Canada's second-largest trading partner (~$100B annual trade)
- **Tensions:** Meng Wanzhou arrest (2018-2021), "Two Michaels" detention (Kovrig, Spavor)
- **Policy:** Canada declared China a "disruptive global power" (2022); shifted to "strategic partner" rhetoric (2024)
- **Critical Minerals:** Canada controls significant rare earth deposits; China dominates processing
- **Human Rights:** Canada raises Xinjiang, Hong Kong, Tibet concerns at UN

**Indo-Pacific Strategy Partners**
- **Japan:** Major trade partner; hydrogen energy cooperation; defense equipment deals
- **South Korea:** Trade in minerals, automotive; cultural exchanges
- **India:** Growing trade; strained by Hardeep Singh Nijjar killing allegations (2023)
- **ASEAN:** Enhanced engagement; ASEAN-Canada strategic partnership (2023)

## 4. Military Capabilities

### Nuclear Status
**Canada is a NON-NUCLEAR STATE.**
- Signed NPT (Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty) in 1969
- No nuclear weapons arsenal; abandoned nuclear weapons programs in the 1950s
- Hosts NO nuclear weapons on Canadian territory
- Allied nuclear umbrella protection through NATO and NORAD

### Canadian Armed Forces (CAF)

| Branch | Personnel | Key Equipment |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Army** | ~23,000 regular | Leopard 2 tanks, LAV 6.0 APCs, artillery systems |
| **Royal Canadian Navy** | ~13,000 regular | 12 Halifax-class frigates, 4 Victoria-class submarines, 12 Harry DeWolf-class Arctic patrol vessels |
| **Royal Canadian Air Force** | ~14,000 regular | CF-188 Hornets (F-18s, being replaced), CC-177 Globemaster, CC-130 Hercules, CP-140 Aurora (P-3 Orion) |
| **Special Forces** | CSOR, JTF2 | Counter-terrorism, special operations |
| **Total Regular Force** | ~68,000 (target 71,500) | |

**Reserve Forces:** ~35,000; Rangers (Arctic): ~5,000

### Major Defense Capabilities

**NORAD Modernization:**
- $38 billion commitment over 20 years for continental defense
- New Arctic over-the-horizon radar systems
- Aerospace surveillance upgrades
- Space-based early warning participation

**Arctic Defense:**
- 12 Harry DeWolf-class Arctic/Offshore Patrol Vessels (6 delivered by 2026)
- Joint Task Force North (Yellowknife)
- Nanisivik Naval Facility (Arctic deep-water port)
- Canadian Rangers — local Arctic defense force

**NATO Commitments:**
- ~1,000 troops in Latvia (eFP Battle Group leadership)
- Ukraine support: $2.5+ billion in military aid (2022-2025)
- NATO battlegroup leadership in Eastern Europe

**Equipment Modernization (2020s):**
- **F-35 Lightning II:** 88 aircraft on order (delivery starting 2026)
- **CSC (Canadian Surface Combatant):** 15 new warships (~$60B program)
- **CC-295 Kingfisher:** Search and rescue aircraft (program cancelled 2022)
- **Fixed-Wing Search and Rescue:** Alternative procurement underway

### Defense Spending
- **2024-25 Budget:** ~$33 billion CAD
- **% of GDP:** ~1.4% (reaching NATO 2% target in 2024)
- **Major Capital Projects:** $100+ billion over 20 years (ships, aircraft, vehicles)
