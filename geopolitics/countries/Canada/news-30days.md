## 🇺🇸 US–Canada Relations — Trade War

### Trump's Tariffs Devastate Canada — Trade War Escalates (February–March 2026)
- **Trump imposed 25% tariffs on Canadian goods** — triggering a full trade war with Canada's largest trading partner.
- **Canada retaliated withCounter-tariffs on US goods** — affecting American products across multiple sectors.
- **Energy sector:** Canada imposing electricity export taxes on US states — directly affecting border communities.
- **Steel and aluminium tariffs** remain in place from earlier rounds.
- **Carney's response:** New PM Mark Carney explicitly stated the relationship with the US has been **"fundamentally altered"** — signalling a long-term structural shift in Canada's trade posture.
- **Buy Canadian campaign** accelerating — Canadian consumers actively shifting away from US products.
- **Supply chain disruptions** affecting Canadian manufacturers who rely on US components.

### Carney: "When Washington Whistles, Ottawa Salutes" — Is Over (March 2026)
- Carney explicitly broke with the traditional doctrine of automatic alignment with US policy.
- **"When Washington whistles, Ottawa salutes"** was the old bilateral motto — Carney declared it dead.
- This marks the most significant shift in Canadian foreign policy in decades.

### Canada Wants to Build Up Its Arctic (March 2026)
- Canada launching a major initiative to **develop its long-neglected Arctic region** — prompted by Russian and Chinese activity in the Arctic.
- Competing interests: Indigenous rights, resource development, sovereignty, and environmental protection all need to be balanced.
- Hard questions being asked about Canada's ability to enforce sovereignty over its northern territories.
