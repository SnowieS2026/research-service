## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### South Africa's Role in Russia-Ukraine Ceasefire Talks — Ramaphosa Mediating (March 2026)
- **President Cyril Ramaphosa positioning South Africa as a neutral mediator** between Russia and the West.
- South Africa hosting **track-2 diplomatic discussions** between Russian and Ukrainian officials in Pretoria.
- Ramaphosa calling for an **immediate ceasefire** — framing South Africa as a non-aligned voice in a polarised conflict.
- **However, South Africa's alignment with Russia and China is increasingly visible** — the ruling ANC's historical ties to Moscow guiding policy.

### South Africa and the Russia-Ukraine War — ANC's Soviet Nostalgia (March 2026)
- **South Africa refusing to condemn Russia's invasion of Ukraine** at the UN — abstaining or voting against Western resolutions.
- Ramaphosa's government claiming neutrality while **culturally and politically aligned with Russia** — a significant tension.
- **South Africa hosting Russian naval exercises** in 2023 — the first time a US-allied country hosted Russia's navy.
- This alignment is **damaging South Africa's relationship with the US and EU** — both key trading partners and donors.

## ⚡ South Africa's Energy Crisis — Eskom and the见 (March 2026)

### Load Shedding — Stage 6 and Beyond (March 2026)
- **South Africa implementing severe load shedding (electricity rationing)** — some of the worst power cuts in years.
- **Eskom** (state electricity company) — collapsing coal-fired power plants, inadequate maintenance, massive debt (~$29B).
- **Eskom debt crisis** — government bailouts straining fiscal budget.
- Ramaphosa government **fast-tracking private power generation** —允许 commercial generators to sell to the grid.

### Renewable Energy Push — Bid Window 7 (March 2026)
- South Africa running **bidding rounds for wind and solar projects** — under the REIPPPP programme.
- **Eskom's transmission network** is the bottleneck — grid connection for new renewable projects delayed.
- South Africa receiving **IPSP (International Partners Group) climate finance** — $8.5B commitment from EU, US, UK to help SA transition away from coal.

## 🏛️ ANC's Decline — 2024 Election Reshaping Politics

### ANC's Wasted Vote — Coalition Government Forming (March 2026)
- **The ANC won only 40% of the vote in 2024** — down from 57% in 2019 — forced to form a coalition.
- **ANC in coalition with the Democratic Alliance (DA)** — the white-led centreright party — a historic compromise.
- **DA leader John Steenhuisen becoming PM** — a historic first for a white leader in post-apartheid South Africa.
- This coalition is **deeply controversial within the ANC** — many ANC members see it as a betrayal of liberation ideals.
- GNU (Government of National Unity) — ANC and DA working together, with others (EFF, IFP) outside government.

### Jacob Zuma's Return — uMkhonto we Sizwe (MK Party) (March 2026)
- **Jacob Zuma's MK Party** won 14% of the vote — a shock result driven by ANC voters who felt betrayed by Ramaphosa.
- **Zuma remains a deeply divisive figure** — convicted of contempt of court, barred from parliament, but politically alive.
- MK Party challenging the GNU from the left — demanding faster land reform, anti-Western rhetoric.

## 🛢️ Mining — South Africa's Critical Minerals Strategy

### Lithium, Nickel, and the Energy Transition (March 2026)
- South Africa sitting on **massive critical mineral reserves** — lithium, manganese, nickel, cobalt, platinum group metals.
- **Platinum group metals (PGMs)** — South Africa produces 70%+ of global supply — critical for hydrogen fuel cells and catalytic converters.
- **Chinese investment in South African mining increasing** — China securing supply chains for the energy transition.
- South Africa balancing **Western demand for "clean" minerals** against Chinese dominance of processing and refining.

### Mining Sector — State Intervention and Black Economic Empowerment (March 2026)
- **Mining Charter** — rules requiring 30% black ownership of mining assets — creating uncertainty for investors.
- **South Africa's mining sector is mature and declining** — deep-level gold and platinum mines becoming uneconomic.
- Illegal mining (**zama zamas**) is a major problem — artisanal miners infiltrating abandoned shafts.

## 🌍 Trade and Sanctions — South Africa Walking a Tightrope (March 2026)

### AGOA — US Trade Benefits at Risk (March 2026)
- **South Africa facing US scrutiny over AGOA (African Growth and Opportunity Act) benefits** — duty-free access to US market.
- US Congress and Trump administration **questioning whether South Africa is a "developing country"** — AGOA benefits may be removed.
- Losing AGOA would hurt South Africa's **automotive exports** (Ford, BMW, VW production in South Africa).
- **South Africa diversifying trade towards China and India** — less dependent on the West than in the past.
