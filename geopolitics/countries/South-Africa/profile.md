## 2. Leadership & Politics

### Current Head of State & Government

**President:** Cyril Ramaphosa (since 15 February 2018)
- Born: 17 November 1952, Soweto
- Background: Anti-apartheid activist, trade union leader, businessman
- ANC President since December 2017
- Former Secretary-General of ANC (1991-1997), Chairperson of Constitutional Assembly
- Negotiator during transition (1990-1994)

### Government of National Unity (GNU) — 2024-2029

Following the **May 2024 elections**, the ANC lost its parliamentary majority for the first time since 1994 (40.2% of vote), necessitating a coalition government:

**Coalition Partners:**

- **Inkatha Freedom Party (IFP)** — 3.4% — Zulu nationalist, center-right
- **Patriotic Alliance (PA)** — 2.3% — Social conservative
- **Freedom Front Plus (VF+)** — 1.4% — Afrikaner nationalist
- **Good Party** — 0.6% — Social democratic

### Key Political Challenges

1. **Coalition Governance** — First post-apartheid coalition; DA-ANC ideological tensions
2. **Corruption** — State capture legacy (Zuma era); ongoing prosecutions
3. **Unemployment** — ~32% official; youth unemployment ~45%
4. **Energy Crisis** — Eskom load shedding; energy transition
5. **Inequality** — Gini coefficient ~0.63 (world's highest)
6. **Crime** — High rates of violent crime, carjacking, farm attacks
7. **Land Reform** — Unresolved expropriation without compensation debate

## 3. Foreign Relations

### Strategic Positioning

South Africa practices **strategic non-alignment**, maintaining relationships across geopolitical blocs while advocating for Global South interests. This creates tension with Western partners while enabling BRICS+ leadership.

### Key Bilateral Relationships

#### **United States** — Complex but Critical
- **Status:** Strained but economically vital
- **Trade:** AGOA beneficiary ($3B exports to US annually)
- **Issues:** 
  - South Africa's perceived pro-Russia stance on Ukraine
  - US allegations of weapons supply to Russia (2023, denied)
  - Israel-Hamas positioning (ICJ case against Israel)
- **Military:** Limited joint exercises; US Africa Command engagement
- **Diplomatic:** Ambassador Reuben Brigety (US) recalled briefly 2023
- **Economic:** Major US companies invested (Microsoft, Google, Ford, BMW)

#### **China** — Primary Economic Partner
- **Trade:** China is South Africa's largest trading partner
  - Exports to China: $26B (platinum, iron ore, manganese, coal)
  - Imports from China: $30B (machinery, electronics, textiles)
- **Investment:** Major Chinese investment in mining, infrastructure
- **BRICS:** Founding member together; supports BRICS expansion
- **Belt & Road:** Signatory (2018)
- **Infrastructure:** Chinese companies building energy, transport projects

#### **Russia** — Historical Ties, Controversial Alignment
- **Historical:** Soviet support for anti-apartheid movement; ANC training
- **Current:** 
  - Refused to condemn Russia's Ukraine invasion at UN
  - Hosted Russian warships for exercises (2023)
  - Accused of supplying weapons (denied)
- **Economic:** Limited trade ($500M annually); nuclear energy discussions
- **Diplomatic:** Supports diplomatic resolution to Ukraine conflict

#### **European Union** — Major Trade Partner
- **Trade Agreement:** EU-SADC EPA (Economic Partnership Agreement)
- **Exports to EU:** ~$20B (vehicles, platinum, wine, fruit)
- **Imports from EU:** ~$25B (machinery, vehicles, pharmaceuticals)
- **Germany:** Largest EU investor; BMW, Mercedes, VW plants
- **UK:** Post-Brexit trade continuity; historical ties

#### **India** — Growing Partnership
- **BRICS Partner:** Founding members together
- **Trade:** ~$15B annually; pharmaceuticals, vehicles, minerals
- **Diaspora:** Large Indian-origin population (1.5M+)
- **Diplomatic:** Shared Commonwealth history; IBSA forum

#### **Israel** — Downgraded Relations
- **Status:** Diplomatic ties downgraded (2018); embassy closed
- **ICJ Case:** South Africa initiated genocide case against Israel (2024)
- **Historical:** Israel had military cooperation with apartheid regime
- **Trade:** Still ~$200M in diamonds, agriculture technology

### Regional Relations (SADC & African Continent)

#### **African Union Leadership**
- **Chair:** South Africa held AU Chair 2020-2021
- **Key Roles:** Peace mediation, COVID-19 vaccine diplomacy
- **AU Commission:** Naledi Pandor (Foreign Minister) sought AU Chair position

#### **Southern African Development Community (SADC)**
- **Founding Member (1980 as SADCC)**
- **Headquarters:** Gaborone, Botswana
- **Role:** Economic integration, peace operations in Mozambique (SADC Mission in Mozambique)
- **Members:** 16 Southern African states

#### **Mozambique** — Security Partnership
- **SADC Mission:** South African troops lead counter-insurgency in Cabo Delgado (since 2021)
- **Insurgency:** Islamist militants linked to ISIS
- **Economic:** Gas pipeline, trade corridor

#### **Zimbabwe** — Complex Ties
- **Historical:** ANC solidarity with ZANU-PF during liberation
- **Current:** Critical of sanctions; advocates normalization
- **Migration:** ~2-3M Zimbabweans in South Africa
- **Trade:** Electricity exports, cross-border commerce

#### **Namibia** — Closest Regional Ally
- **Historical:** SWAPO solidarity; Namibia's liberation supported by South Africa's ANC
- **Trade:** Integrated economies; shared currency peg
- **Diplomatic:** Close alignment on regional issues

## 4. Military Capabilities

### Nuclear Status

**Historical Nuclear Weapons Program (ENDED)**
- **Program:** Developed 6 nuclear weapons (1978-1990)
- **Termination:** Voluntarily dismantled 1991-1993
- **NPT:** Acceded 1991
- **Current Status:** Non-nuclear weapon state; no arsenal
- **Uranium Enrichment:** Closed in 1990s

**Current Nuclear Position:**
- Strong advocate for nuclear disarmament
- Signatory to Treaty of Pelindaba (African Nuclear-Weapon-Free Zone)
- Opposes nuclear proliferation by any state

### South African National Defence Force (SANDF)

**Active Personnel:** ~75,000
**Reserves:** ~12,000

| Branch | Personnel | Key Equipment |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Army** | ~40,000 | Olifant tanks, Ratel IFVs, Rooikat armored cars |
| **Navy** | ~7,000 | 4 Valour-class frigates, 3 Heroine-class submarines (Type 209) |
| **Air Force** | ~10,000 | Gripen fighters (26), Hawk trainers, C-130 transport, Oryx helicopters |
| **Military Health** | ~8,000 | Medical services |
| **Special Forces** | ~3,000 | 1, 4, 5 SF Regiments; Reconnaissance Commando |

**Budget:** ~$4.5B USD (2024) — 1.2% of GDP

### Defense Industry

**Denel SOC Ltd** — State-owned defense conglomerate
- **Products:** Rooivalk attack helicopter, G5/G6 artillery, missiles
- **Exports:** UAVs, ammunition, armored vehicles
- **Status:** Financial crisis; restructuring

**Armscor** — Arms procurement agency

### Peacekeeping Operations

South Africa is a major African peacekeeping contributor:

| Mission | Location | Contribution |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| **MONUSCO** | DRC | ~1,300 troops (ended 2024) |
| **UNMISS** | South Sudan | ~800 troops |
| **SADC Mission** | Mozambique | ~1,500 troops |
| **MINUSMA** | Mali | Ended 2023 |

**Doctrine:** "African solutions to African problems"; emphasis on continental peace operations

### Military Challenges

1. **Funding** — Budget below needed levels; equipment aging
2. **Maintenance** — Readiness issues across services
3. **Personnel** — HIV/AIDS impact; skills retention
4. **Peacekeeping Costs** — UN reimbursements insufficient

## 7. Historical Context

### Pre-Colonial Era

| Period | Key Events |
|--------|------------|
| **Pre-1652** | Indigenous peoples: Khoisan, Bantu-speaking nations (Zulu, Xhosa, Sotho, Tswana) |
| **1652** | Dutch East India Company establishes Cape Colony |
| **1806** | British take control of Cape Colony |
| **1830s** | Great Trek; Voortrekkers establish Boer republics |
| **1879** | Anglo-Zulu War; Zulu Kingdom defeated |
| **1880-1881** | First Boer War; British defeat |
| **1899-1902** | Second Boer War; British victory, concentration camps |

### Union of South Africa (1910-1948)

- **1910:** Union formed (British dominion)
- **1913:** Native Land Act — 8% of land for Black population
- **1948:** National Party wins election; apartheid begins

### Apartheid Era (1948-1994)

**Key Laws:**
- **Population Registration Act (1950)** — Racial classification
- **Group Areas Act (1950)** — Segregated living areas
- **Pass Laws** — Movement restrictions
- **Bantu Education Act (1953)** — Inferior education for Black population
- **Homelands (Bantustans)** — "Independent" states for ethnic groups

**Resistance:**
- **1912:** South African Native National Congress (later ANC) formed
- **1944:** ANC Youth League (Mandela, Tambo, Sisulu)
- **1952:** Defiance Campaign
- **1960:** Sharpeville Massacre — 69 killed; ANC banned
- **1961:** Umkhonto we Sizwe (MK) formed — armed wing
- **1962:** Mandela arrested; Rivonia Trial (1963-64)
- **1976:** Soweto Uprising — student protests
- **1980s:** International sanctions; internal resistance grows

**International Pressure:**
- UN arms embargo (1977)
- Economic sanctions (divestment movement)
- Cultural boycott
- Sports boycott (Olympics, cricket, rugby bans)

### Transition (1990-1994)

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **2 Feb 1990** | F.W. de Klerk unbans ANC, PAC, SACP; releases Mandela |
| **11 Feb 1990** | Nelson Mandela released after 27 years |
| **1991-1993** | Negotiations (CODESA); violence threatens process |
| **Apr 1994** | First democratic elections |
| **10 May 1994** | Nelson Mandela inaugurated as President |

### Post-Apartheid Era (1994-Present)

**Presidents:**
| President | Term | Key Events |
|-----------|------|------------|
| **Nelson Mandela** | 1994-1999 | Truth and Reconciliation Commission; constitution; reconciliation |
| **Thabo Mbeki** | 1999-2008 | Economic growth; HIV/AIDS controversy; NEPAD |
| **Kgalema Motlanthe** | 2008-2009 | Interim president |
| **Jacob Zuma** | 2009-2018 | State capture; corruption scandals; Gupta family influence |
| **Cyril Ramaphosa** | 2018-present | Zondo Commission; COVID-19; 2021 riots; 2024 coalition |

### Major Turning Points

1. **1995 Rugby World Cup** — National unity moment (Invictus)
2. **1996 Constitution** — World's most progressive constitution
3. **1998 Truth and Reconciliation Report** — Documented atrocities
4. **2008 Power Crisis** — Load shedding begins
5. **2012 Marikana Massacre** — Police kill 34 miners; labor unrest
6. **2016 State Capture Report** — Zuma era corruption exposed
7. **2018 Zuma Resignation** — Ramaphosa takes power
8. **2020 COVID-19** — Hard lockdown; economic damage
9. **2021 July Riots** — Zuma arrest triggers unrest; 350+ killed
10. **2024 ANC <50%** — First coalition government
