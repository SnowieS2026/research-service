## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Nigeria's Security Crisis — Multi-Front Insurgency (March 2026)
- Nigeria facing **multiple simultaneous security threats** — the most complex security environment in its history.
- **Boko Haram and ISWAP** — still active in the Northeast (Borno, Yobe states) despite a decade of military operations.
- **Banditry** — armed criminal gangs kidnapping, raiding, and displacing communities across the North-West and North-Central regions.
- **IPOB/ESN** — the Indigenous People of Biafra's armed wing demanding a Biafran secession in the South-East — Nigerian military operations causing civilian casualties.
- **Niger Delta militants** — resurgent attacks on oil infrastructure in the Niger Delta.

### Military Operations Against Bandits — Niger State (March 2026)
- Nigerian military launching **sweeping operations against bandit hideouts** in the North-West.
- Airforce bombing bandit camps — but ground operations complicated by terrain and informants leaking to bandits.

## 🇳🇬 Niger–France–US — The Sahel Pivot (March 2026)

### Niger Junta Expelling French and US Forces (2023–2026)
- **Niger expelled French forces** in 2023 — France withdrawing from its largest Sahel counter-terrorism base.
- **Niger has turned toward Russia** — Russian military advisors and Wagner Group personnel have entered Niger.
- Nigeria caught in the middle — a Russian-backed military junta to its north, while Nigeria maintains US and French security partnerships.
- **Nigeria hosting US drone operations** from Niger — the Pentagon's AFRICOM basing strategy reliant on Nigerien territory.

### ECOWAS — Nigeria's Regional Leadership Challenged (March 2026)
- Nigeria is the **dominant ECOWAS power** — contributing 50% of the organisation's budget.
- ECOWAS successfully negotiated with the Niger junta in 2023 — averting military intervention at the last moment.
- Nigeria continuing to project security across West Africa — **military interventions in Gambia (2017) and Togo** in recent years.

## 🛢️ Oil — Nigeria's Critical Resource Under Threat (March 2026)

### Niger Delta Militants Targeting Oil Infrastructure (March 2026)
- **Oil production disrupted** by militant attacks in the Niger Delta.
- Nigeria is Africa's **largest oil producer** — but sabotage and theft are chronic problems.
- **Dangote Refinery operational** — Nigeria now refining most of its own crude oil domestically — a major strategic shift.
- Nigeria still dependent on oil exports for foreign currency — despite diversification efforts.

## 🇳🇪 Tinubu's Economic Reforms — Painful but Productive (March 2026)

### Fuel Subsidy Removal — Economic Shock (2023–2026)
- **President Bola Tinubu removed the fuel subsidy** in May 2023 — immediately causing fuel prices to double overnight.
- The subsidy was a massive fiscal burden — saving Nigeria ~$10B/year but creating an entrenched black market.
- **Inflation surged to 33%+** — the removal caused immediate pain for ordinary Nigerians.
- Despite the economic shock, Tinubu's reform agenda is credited with stabilising Nigeria's currency (naira) and attracting IMF/World Bank support.

### IMF Programme — Nigeria on an IMF Watch (March 2026)
- Nigeria negotiating with the **IMF** for a loan programme — to plug its fiscal deficit and restore investor confidence.
- IMF conditions include **fiscal consolidation, subsidy reforms, and exchange rate liberalisation**.

## 🌊 Dangote Refinery — Nigeria's Industrial Gambit (March 2026)

### Dangote Refinery — West Africa's Largest (March 2026)
- **Africa's largest oil refinery** — built by Aliko Dangote, Africa's richest person — is now operational.
- The 650,000 barrel-per-day refinery can meet all of Nigeria's fuel needs and export surplus.
- This is transforming Nigeria's **petroleum trade balance** — reducing the need for fuel imports.
- Nigeria previously exported crude and imported refined fuel — an absurd arrangement that the Dangote Refinery is ending.

## 🏛️ 2027 Elections — Succession Politics Heating Up (March 2026)

### Tinubu's Age and Succession (March 2026)
- President Tinubu is **82 years old** — speculation about his health and ability to serve a full term.
- **APC primaries in 2027** will determine who succeeds Tinubu — if he runs, and if he finishes his term.
- Political succession is Nigeria's most significant **political risk** — past transitions have been accompanied by electoral violence.
