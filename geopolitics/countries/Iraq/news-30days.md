## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Iran's War with Israel — Iraq Caught Between the US and Iran (March 2026)
- **Iran-backed Shia militias in Iraq have launched attacks on US bases** in Iraq and Syria — several US soldiers wounded in Kata'ib Hezbollah and Asa'ib Ahl al-Haq strikes.
- Iraq's government, nominally independent but deeply influenced by Iran, faces an impossible position: the US provides military assistance that Iraq needs against ISIS, but Iran-backed militias control significant political power.
- **US forces remain in Iraq** (~2,500 troops) — the US-Iraq security agreement, renewed in September 2024, has a complicated withdrawal timeline.
- Iraq's airspace is being used by multiple parties — Iranian missiles, Israeli counter-strikes, and US aircraft all operating in Iraqi airspace.

### Iran-Syria Corridor — Post-Assad Changes (Late 2024–2026)
- **The fall of the Assad regime in December 2024** fundamentally changed Iraq's strategic position.
- The **Iranian land corridor** from Iran through Iraq to Syria and Lebanon — Iran's primary supply route to its regional proxies — was disrupted when Assad fell.
- Iran has been working to **re-establish supply routes** through new channels, but the corridor is less reliable.
- Iraq's new government is trying to **distance itself from Iranian dominance** while not provoking Iran enough to destabilise the country.

## 🇺🇸 US Troops — Withdrawal Agreement Under Strain (March 2026)

### September 2024 Agreement — US Withdrawal Timeline (March 2026)
- **Under the September 2024 agreement, US forces were to withdraw from Iraq** — but the timeline has been complicated by the Iran-Israel conflict.
- Iraq's government wants the US to leave — politically popular, particularly with Shia factions.
- But Iraqi military leaders privately acknowledge their forces cannot yet fill the security gap left by a US withdrawal.
- **Iranian-backed militias watching closely** — a full US withdrawal would significantly strengthen Iran's regional position.

## 🛢️ Iraq Development Road — $17B Strategic Project

### Iraq's Development Road Project (March 2026)
- **Iraq is developing a $17 billion "Development Road"** — a rail and highway link from the Persian Gulf through Iraq to Turkey and Europe.
- The project, involving Turkey, UAE, and Qatar, aims to make Iraq a key transit hub — competing with Iran's port access.
- This is part of Iraq's strategy to **reduce dependence on Iranian economic dominance** and develop alternative trade routes.
- Iran may view this project as a strategic challenge to its regional influence.

## ⚔️ ISIS — Still a Threat Despite Defeat

### ISIS Resurgence in Iraq (March 2026)
- ISIS sleeper cells remain active in Iraq — particularly in the **Sunni-majority provinces of Anbar, Nineveh, and Salah ad Din**.
- Iraqi security forces, with US support, continue counter-ISIS operations — but the political fragmentation makes a comprehensive security strategy difficult.
- The **Kurdish Peshmerga** (in the north) and Iraqi Army forces maintain an uneasy coordination against ISIS remnants.

## 🏛️ Domestic Politics — Iraqi Government Fragile

### Sudani Government — Balancing Act (March 2026)
- PM Mohammed Shia al-Sudani's government continuing to balance Iranian-backed Shia militias, Kurdish regional government, and Sunni political blocs.
- **Corruption remains endemic** — Iraq ranks near the bottom of Transparency International's Corruption Perceptions Index.
- **Oil wealth distribution** remains the central political dispute — between Baghdad, the Kurdistan Region, and provincial governments.

---

## 🛢️ Economy

- Iraq is **5th largest oil producer globally** — 145 billion barrels of reserves.
- Oil exports generating significant revenue, but **corruption and mismanagement** mean limited benefits reach ordinary citizens.
- Iraq still importing refined petroleum products — its domestic refining capacity insufficient for domestic needs.
- Water scarcity worsening — the Tigris and Euphrates rivers are over-allocated, and climate change is reducing water flows.
