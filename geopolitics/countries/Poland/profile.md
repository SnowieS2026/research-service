## 7. Historical Context

### Pre-World War II

**Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth (1569-1795):** At its peak (16th-17th century), Poland was the largest state in Europe — a multi-ethnic, multi-religious democracy (nobles' democracy) stretching from Baltic to Black Sea.

**Partitions (1772, 1793, 1795):** Poland was erased from the map for 123 years, partitioned between Russia, Prussia (Germany), and Austria. Poles staged multiple failed uprisings (1830, 1863).

**World War I (1914-1918):** Poland regained independence in 1918 after 123 years of partition. Józef Piłsudski led the new state.

**Interwar Period (1918-1939):** Poland fought border wars with Soviet Russia (1919-1921, won at Battle of Warsaw), Ukraine, Lithuania, Czechoslovakia. The 1935 constitution established authoritarian "Sanation" regime.

---

### World War II (1939-1945)

**Invasion (Sept 1, 1939):** Germany invaded Poland, triggering WWII. Soviet Union invaded from east on Sept 17 (Molotov-Ribbenshaw Pact).

**Occupation:** Poland suffered the worst per-capita losses of any country:
- **6 million killed** — ~17% of population (including ~3 million Polish Jews, ~90% of Jewish population)
- **Warsaw Uprising (1944)** — Crushed by Germans, 200,000 civilians killed, city razed
- **Holocaust:** Auschwitz-Birkenau, Treblinka, Sobibor, Belzec, Majdanek — major extermination camps located in Poland
- **Katyn Massacre (1940):** NKVD murdered 22,000 Polish officers/intellectuals

**Resistance:** Polish Home Army (Armia Krajowa) was the largest resistance in occupied Europe (~400,000). Warsaw Ghetto Uprising (1943) was the first urban uprising against Germans.

**Post-War:** Poland lost eastern territories (to USSR) and gained western territories (from Germany) — borders shifted 200km westward.

---

### Communist Era (1945-1989)

**Soviet Puppet State:** Poland became a Soviet satellite under communist rule (Polish United Workers' Party).

**Stalinist Era (1948-1956):** Soviet-style repression, collectivization, show trials. Cardinal Wyszyński imprisoned. Church persecuted.

**Thaw (1956-1970s):** Władysław Gomułka's "Polish October" brought limited liberalization. But 1968 saw anti-Semitic purges (Jewish expulsion).

**Solidarity Movement (1980):** 
- **August 1980:** Gdańsk Shipyard strikes led by Lech Wałęsa
- **Solidarity (Solidarność)** — First independent trade union in Soviet bloc, 10 million members
- **Martial Law (1981-1983):** General Jaruzelski crushed Solidarity, but movement survived underground
- **1989 Round Table Talks:** Negotiated transition to democracy — first in Soviet bloc

**June 4, 1989 Elections:** Solidarity won 161/161 contested seats — beginning of the end for communism in Poland.

---

### Third Polish Republic (1989-Present)

**Economic Shock Therapy:** Finance Minister Leszek Balcerowicz implemented radical free-market reforms (1989-1991) — rapid privatization, liberalization. Painful but successful — Poland became fastest-growing post-communist economy.

**NATO Membership (1999):** Poland joined NATO in first post-communist wave (with Czechia, Hungary).

**EU Membership (2004):** Poland joined EU with 9 other countries (biggest enlargement). EU funds fueled infrastructure development.

**Democratic Backsliding (2015-2023):** Law and Justice (PiS) government undermined judicial independence, media freedom, and EU rule-of-law norms — leading to EU Article 7 procedure and frozen funds.

**Return to Pro-EU Government (2023):** Donald Tusk's coalition ousted PiS, restoring EU relations and judicial reforms.
