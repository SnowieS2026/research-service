## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### India-Pakistan Conflict — Ceasefire Agreed (April 2025, Ongoing Tension)
- **India and Pakistan agreed to a ceasefire** following the April-May 2025 crisis — the most serious escalation between the two nuclear-armed neighbours in decades.
- The ceasefire was reportedly **brokered with US, UK, and Saudi involvement** — though tensions remain high along the Line of Control (LoC).
- **Both countries pulled back from the brink** of a potential nuclear exchange — the first time two nuclear-armed states have engaged in direct military conflict in the nuclear era.
- India claims to have inflicted significant damage on Pakistani territory during the conflict — Pakistan disputes this.
- The ceasefire remains fragile — both sides have accused each other of violations.

- **India holds the BRICS chair** — navigating significant internal divisions within the bloc over the US-Israel strikes on Iran.
- China and Russia were more sympathetic to Iran; India and Brazil took a more cautious approach — highlighting BRICS' lack of strategic coherence.

## 🇨🇳 India–China Relations

### Tensions Along the Line of Actual Control (LAC) — October 2024 Agreement (Still Fragile)
- **India and China agreed on a border patrol framework** in October 2024 — reducing tensions along the disputed Himalayan border.
- However, the agreement is **incomplete** — significant areas of the LAC remain unresolved.
- **China's PLA** continues to build infrastructure on its side of the border — dual-use roads, airfields, and troop positions.
- India has increased its own border infrastructure spending significantly.
- The **economic relationship continues to grow** despite political tensions — Chinese goods still flowing into India through third-country routes.

## 🇷🇺 India–Russia Relations — Arms and Energy

### Russia–India Arms Trade — S-400 and BrahMos (March 2026)
- **Russia has delivered S-400 air defence missile systems to India** — a $5.4B deal that has frustrated the US, which sanctioned India under CAATSA for the purchase.
- **BrahMos cruise missiles** — the India-Russia joint venture continuing to produce supersonic cruise missiles for the Indian military.
- Russia historically India's largest arms supplier — but this is changing as India diversifies to US, Israeli, and domestic sources.
- **Crude oil imports from Russia surged** from 2% to 36% of India's oil imports since 2022 — India taking advantage of discounted Russian crude that European buyers rejected.
- This pragmatic approach to Russia reflects India's **multi-alignment strategy** — maintaining ties with Moscow while deepening security cooperation with the US.

## 🇺🇸 Trump Tariffs — India Caught in the Trade War (March 2026)

### Trump Imposes 50% Tariffs on India — Negotiations Ongoing
- **Trump imposed 50% tariffs on Indian goods** entering the United States — a major blow to India's export economy.
- India was already negotiating with the Trump administration to avoid tariffs — discussions continuing.
- India's **proposed deal: reduce tariffs to 18%** in exchange for US tariff relief — the deal is reportedly close but not yet finalised.
- India retaliating with tariffs on some US goods — though India's market is smaller than the US market.
- **Tech sector particularly affected** — India's IT services exports to the US facing new pressure.

## 🌊 Indian Ocean — Regional Dominance

### Malabar Exercises — QUAD Navies (October 2025)
- India hosting the **Malabar naval exercise** — the premier QUAD naval drill involving the US, Japan, Australia, and India.
- Exercise demonstrating freedom of navigation and Indo-Pacific security cooperation.
- **Indian Navy expanding its reach** — deep-water ports, maritime surveillance aircraft, and submarine capabilities growing.
- India positioning itself as the **Indian Ocean's dominant power** — competing with China's expanding naval presence in the Bay of Bengal and Indian Ocean.

### Sri Lanka–India–China Triangle (March 2026)
- **Sri Lanka** remains a key arena for India-China competition.

- India countering with its own development projects and economic engagement.
- The **Hambantota Port** — leased to a Chinese company — remains a sore point in India's strategic calculus.
