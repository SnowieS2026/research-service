## 2. Leadership & Politics

### Current Government (2024-2029)

**Prime Minister:** Narendra Modi (Bharatiya Janata Party - BJP)
- Third consecutive term (2014-2019, 2019-2024, 2024-2029)
- First PM since Jawaharlal Nehru to win three consecutive terms
- 2024 election: BJP lost outright majority (240 seats, down from 303); now leads NDA coalition (293 seats)
- Key shift: Must now rely on coalition partners (NDA allies: TDP, JD(U), others)

**Key Cabinet Ministers:**
- **External Affairs:** Subrahmanyam Jaishankar (continues from previous term)
- **Defence:** Rajnath Singh
- **Finance:** Nirmala Sitharaman
- **Home Affairs:** Amit Shah

### Political Landscape

**Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP):**
- Right-wing, Hindu nationalist party
- Ideology: Hindutva (Hindu cultural nationalism)
- Dominated Indian politics since 2014
- Faces increasing coalition constraints after 2024

**Indian National Congress (INC):**
- Center-left, secular party
- Historical dominance (1947-2014, with interruptions)
- Current opposition leader
- 2024 manifesto emphasized harder line on China, greater engagement with neighbors

**Regional Parties:**
- Increasing influence in coalition politics
- Key players: TDP (Andhra Pradesh), JD(U) (Bihar), DMK (Tamil Nadu), TMC (West Bengal)
- State governments crucial for foreign investment and policy implementation

### Democratic Backsliding Concerns
- 2021: Freedom House downgraded India's democracy rating from "Free" to "Partly Free"
- 2024: Press Freedom Index ranked India 159th out of 180 countries
- 2019: Citizenship Amendment Act introduced religion-based criteria for citizenship (large protests)
- 2019: Removal of special autonomy for Indian-administered Kashmir (Article 370)
- Subsequent lockdowns, communication restrictions, and increased military presence in Kashmir

### Foreign Policy Doctrine
**"Strategic Autonomy"** — Core principle since independence:
- Non-alignment during Cold War (founding member of Non-Aligned Movement)
- Multi-alignment in contemporary era: engaging all major powers without formal alliances
- "Friend to all, enemy to none" approach
- External Affairs Minister Jaishankar's doctrine: "Engage America, manage China, cultivate Europe, reassure Russia, bring Japan into play, draw neighbors in, extend the neighborhood"

## 3. Foreign Relations

### Major Powers

#### United States
**Relationship Status:** Strategic partnership, deepening but transactional

- **QUAD Member:** India, US, Japan, Australia — Indo-Pacific security architecture
- **Defense:** Foundational defense agreements (LEMOA, COMCASA, BECA); increasing interoperability
- **Trade:** $120 billion bilateral trade (FY 2023-24); US is India's 2nd largest goods trade partner
- **Technology:** iCET (Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technology) — semiconductors, AI, quantum
- **Tensions:**
  - 2025: Trump tariffs up to 50% on Indian goods
  - Early 2026: Trade deal proposed to reduce tariffs to 18%; India agreed to stop buying Russian oil (unconfirmed implementation)
  - India's continued Russian defense purchases
  - US response to April-May 2025 India-Pakistan conflict viewed as disappointing by New Delhi

#### China
**Relationship Status:** Complex rivalry — economic partner, strategic adversary

- **Border Disputes:** 3,500 km disputed border (Line of Actual Control)
- **2020 Galwan Valley Clash:** Deadliest encounter in decades (20 Indian soldiers, 4+ Chinese killed)
- **October 2024:** Border management agreement signed; partial troop pullback
- **Trade Paradox:** China is India's largest trading partner ($147 billion FY 2023-24); massive trade deficit (5:1)
- **Strategic Encirclement:** China's Belt and Road Initiative (BRI), China-Pakistan Economic Corridor ($62 billion), Gwadar port
- **Indian Ocean:** Growing Chinese naval presence — research vessels, submarine deployments
- **Dalai Lama:** India hosts Tibetan government-in-exile; succession concerns (Dalai Lama, 89, may create leadership schism)

#### Russia
**Relationship Status:** Long-standing partnership, pragmatic hedge against China

- **Historical Ties:** Cold War ally; primary defense supplier for decades
- **Defense:** ~65% of Indian military equipment of Russian/Soviet origin
- **Energy:** Russian oil imports grew from 2% to ~36% of crude imports by 2024 (post-Ukraine invasion)
- **Arms Exports:** India constitutes nearly 10% of global arms imports (2019-2023)
- **Strategic Value:** Russia as continental counterweight to China; UNSC veto power
- **Tensions:** Sanctions concerns; Russia's increasing dependence on China weakens its utility

## 7. Historical Context

- **British East India Company Rule (1757-1858):** Trade exploitation → political control
- **British Raj (1858-1947):** Crown colony; economic extraction (textiles deindustrialization)
- **Independence Movement:** Mahatma Gandhi's non-violent resistance; Indian National Congress
- **Partition (1947):** Division into Hindu-majority India, Muslim-majority Pakistan
- **Human Cost:** ~1-2 million deaths; 10-15 million displaced

### Post-Independence Period

**Jawaharlal Nehru Era (1947-1964):**
- Democratic socialist economic model
- Non-Aligned Movement founding (1955 Bandung Conference)
- Secular, pluralist vision for India
- Border conflict with China (1962) — decisive defeat

- Green Revolution (agricultural self-sufficiency)
