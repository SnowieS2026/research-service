## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Prabowo's Free and Active Doctrine — Indonesia's Balancing Act (March 2026)
- President **Prabowo Subianto** continuing Indonesia's traditional "Free and Active" (bebas-aktif) foreign policy — refusing to be drawn into great power rivalries.
- Indonesia positioning itself as a **neutral mediator** between the US/Israel and Iran — offering to broker dialogue.
- Prabowo visiting multiple world capitals in his first months in office — demonstrating Indonesia's desire for global influence beyond ASEAN.

### Indonesia's Stance on the Iran-Israel War (March 2026)
- Indonesia, as the world's largest Muslim-majority nation, has **condemned Israeli aggression against Iran** — but stopped short of providing material support.
- Indonesia calling for an immediate ceasefire — while maintaining diplomatic channels with both sides.
- The conflict has reignited Indonesian domestic political pressure on the government to take a stronger stance against Israel.

## 🇨🇳 China Relations — Strategic Rivalry in the South China Sea

### Indonesia and China — Fishing Boat Incident (March 2026)
- **Indonesia's navy confronting Chinese coast guard vessels** near the Natuna Sea — Chinese fishing boats operating in waters Indonesia claims as its Exclusive Economic Zone.
- Indonesia has expelled Chinese vessels on multiple occasions — tensions running high.
- **Natuna Sea** — at the southern edge of the South China Sea — is a key flashpoint between Indonesia and China.

### Indonesia's Military Modernisation — Prabowo's Defence Push (March 2026)
- Prabowo, a former military general, has made **defence modernisation** a centrepiece of his government.
- Indonesia buying **French Rafale jets, US F-15EX aircraft, and Danish F-16s** — diversifying away from any single supplier.
- Indonesia's **defence budget ($15.1B, 1.05% of GDP)** — still relatively low but growing.

## 🇮🇩 Domestic Politics — Prabowo's Government

### Human Rights Concerns — Prabowo's Past (March 2026)
- Prabowo has a **controversial human rights record** — he was once banned from entering the US under a ban on foreign officials involved in gross human rights violations.
- Human rights groups watching his government closely — concerns about democratic backsliding.
- His administration has promised an investigation into past human rights abuses but critics say progress is slow.

### Food Security — Indonesia's Rice Crisis (March 2026)
- Indonesia, the world's 4th largest rice producer, facing pressure from **El Niño weather patterns** affecting harvests.
- Prabowo has made food self-sufficiency a key policy priority — investing in irrigation, seeds, and fertilisers.
- Indonesia still a **net importer of rice** — a vulnerability in a geopolitically uncertain world.

## 🌏 ASEAN — Indonesia's Regional Leadership

### ASEAN Unity Under Pressure — South China Sea (March 2026)
- Indonesia using its ASEAN chairmanship to push for a **unified ASEAN position on the South China Sea** — but divisions remain between claimants (Vietnam, Philippines, Malaysia) and others (Cambodia, Laos) more aligned with China.
- Indonesia pushing for **COC (Code of Conduct) negotiations** with China — progress slow.
- ASEAN continuing to be a low-commitment organisation — Indonesia leading but unable to force consensus.

### Papua Region — Security Crackdown (March 2026)
- Indonesia continuing military operations in the **Papua region** — human rights groups reporting civilian casualties.
- Indonesia controls the western half of New Guinea — the Free Papua movement seeks independence.
- Military presence increasing as Indonesia develops Papua's natural resources.

## 🛢️ Economy — Nickel and the Energy Transition

### Indonesia's Nickel Dominance — Battery Supply Chain (March 2026)
- **Indonesia controls roughly 50% of global nickel supply** — a critical mineral for electric vehicle batteries.
- Chinese companies have invested heavily in Indonesian nickel processing — Chinese firms dominate Indonesia's nickel sector.
- Indonesia's strategy: process nickel domestically, attract EV battery and stainless steel manufacturers.
- Tesla, LG, and Hyundai investing in Indonesian nickel processing plants.
- Indonesia also moving into **electric vehicle manufacturing** — targeting the ASEAN market.
