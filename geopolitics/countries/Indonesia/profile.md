## 2. Leadership & Politics

### Current President: Prabowo Subianto (born October 17, 1951)

**Background:**
- Son of Sumitro Djojohadikusumo, economist and minister under Sukarno and Suharto
- Grandson of Margono Djojohadikusumo, founder of Bank Negara Indonesia (BNI)
- Educated at Victoria Institution (Kuala Lumpur) and American School in London
- Graduated from Indonesian Military Academy (Akademi Militer) in 1974
- Former Lieutenant General in Indonesian Army; served in Kopassus (Special Forces) and Kostrad (Strategic Reserve)
- Discharged from military in 1998 for role in kidnapping democracy activists during Suharto's fall
- Previously served as Minister of Defence (2019-2024) under President Jokowi

**Political Career:**
- Founded Gerindra Party in 2008
- Ran for Vice President (2009), President (2014, 2019, 2024)
- Won 2024 presidential election with 58.59% of vote in first round
- Third president with military background (after Suharto and Yudhoyono)
- Oldest first-term president in Indonesian history (73 at inauguration)

**Key Controversies:**
- Human rights allegations from East Timor operations (1976-1998)
- Role in kidnappings of democracy activists (1997-1998)
- Allegations of involvement in May 1998 riots
- Dismissal from military for "misinterpreting orders"
- Previously banned from entering United States due to human rights concerns

### Government Structure

**Executive:**
- President serves as both head of state and government
- Directly elected for 5-year terms (maximum 2 terms)
- Prabowo heads the "Red and White Cabinet" (Kabinet Merah Putih)

**Legislature:**
- People's Consultative Assembly (MPR) - bicameral
  - Regional Representative Council (DPD) - upper house
  - House of Representatives (DPR) - lower house (580 seats)

**Judiciary:**
- Supreme Court (Mahkamah Agung)
- Constitutional Court (Mahkamah Konstitusi)

### Political Parties (2024 Election)
- **Gerindra Party** - Prabowo's party, nationalist-conservative
- **PDI-P** (Indonesian Democratic Party of Struggle) - Megawati's party, secular-nationalist
- **Golkar** - Former ruling party under Suharto, centrist
- **PKS** (Prosperous Justice Party) - Islamist-oriented
- **Democratic Party** (Demokrat) - Yudhoyono's party, centrist-liberal
- **PKB** (National Awakening Party) - Islamist-leaning, Nahdlatul Ulama affiliated
- **PAN** (National Mandate Party) - Centrist, Muhammadiyah affiliated

## 3. Foreign Relations

### Core Doctrine: "Free and Active" (Bebas Aktif)

Indonesia's foreign policy is guided by the principle of "free and active" (bebas aktif), established by founding Vice President Mohammad Hatta. This doctrine emphasizes:
- Non-alignment with any major power bloc
- Active engagement in international affairs
- Independence in foreign policy decisions
- Rejection of foreign military bases on Indonesian territory
- Commitment to peaceful resolution of disputes

### Key Bilateral Relationships

#### United States
- **Status:** Strategic partner since 2015; comprehensive strategic partnership under negotiation
- **Trade:** US is Indonesia's 4th largest trading partner
- **Security:** Annual military exercises (Garuda Shield, Cooperation Afloat Readiness and Training)
- **Tensions:** Historical human rights concerns over Prabowo's past; US arms sales restrictions
- **2024-25:** Prabowo visited Washington in November 2024; seeking technology transfer and investment
- **Key interests:** Critical minerals, supply chain diversification, Indo-Pacific security

#### China
- **Status:** Comprehensive strategic partnership (2013)
- **Trade:** China is Indonesia's largest trading partner and top investor
- **Investment:** Major infrastructure projects under Belt and Road Initiative
- **Contentious issues:** South China Sea disputes (Natuna Islands), debt dependency concerns
- **2024-25:** Prabowo visited Beijing days after inauguration; pledged to continue Jokowi-era cooperation
- **Economic ties:** Nickel processing, high-speed rail (Jakarta-Bandung), industrial parks

#### Australia
- **Status:** Comprehensive strategic partnership; only such agreement for Australia in ASEAN
- **Trade:** IA-CEPA free trade agreement (2020)
- **Security:** Lombok Treaty (2006), defense cooperation, intelligence sharing
- **Historical tensions:** Papuan independence, spying scandals (2013), executions of Australian drug smugglers
- **Current:** Prabowo maintains warm relationship with Australia; first bilateral visit as President-elect
- **Key cooperation:** Counter-terrorism, maritime security, people smuggling

#### India
- **Status:** Comprehensive strategic partnership (2022)
- **Trade:** Growing trade in defense equipment, palm oil, coal
- **Security:** Defense cooperation, joint military exercises
- **Connectivity:** Direct flight links, cultural exchanges
- **Strategic:** Both concerned about China's maritime expansion
- **2024-25:** Prabowo inaugural visit included India; discussed defense and food security

#### Japan
- **Status:** Strategic partnership
- **Trade:** Major investor, automobile manufacturing, infrastructure
- **Development:** Long-running ODA program, capacity building
- **2024-25:** Japan remains key partner for infrastructure development

#### ASEAN Member States
- **Role:** Founding member (1967)
- **Stance:** ASEAN centrality in regional architecture
- **Key relationships:** Close ties with Singapore (investment hub), Malaysia (trade), Thailand
- **Challenges:** South China Sea mediation, Myanmar crisis response

### Regional Security Architecture

**ASEAN:** Core pillar of Indonesia's foreign policy
- Champion of ASEAN centrality and unity
- Led negotiations on South China Sea Code of Conduct
- Principle of non-interference in member states' affairs

**ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF):** Indonesia participates in regional security dialogue

**Indian Ocean Rim Association (IORA):** Founding member, promotes economic cooperation

### United Nations & Multilateral

- UN member since 1950
- Non-Aligned Movement member (hosted 1955 Bandung Conference)
- G20 member (hosted 2022 summit in Bali)
- Organisation of Islamic Cooperation member
- Largest contributor to UN peacekeeping missions in ASEAN (24,000+ personnel since 1957)

## 7. Historical Context

### Pre-Independence

**Early History:**
- Homo erectus ("Java Man") fossils from ~500,000 BCE
- Austronesian migration ~2000 BCE
- Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms (Srivijaya 7th-13th century, Majapahit 13th-15th century)
- Islamic sultanates emerge from 13th century

**Colonial Period:**
- Portuguese arrival early 16th century
- Dutch East India Company (VOC) established 1602
- Dutch colonial rule (Dutch East Indies) 1800-1942
- Japanese occupation 1942-1945

### Independence and Sukarno Era (1945-1967)

**National Revolution (1945-1949):**
- August 17, 1945: Sukarno proclaims independence
- Armed struggle against Dutch attempts to reassert control
- International pressure forces Dutch recognition December 27, 1949

**Sukarno Presidency (1945-1967):**
- First President, founding father
- "Guided Democracy" (1959) - authoritarian shift
- Non-Aligned Movement champion
- 1955 Bandung Conference
- Confrontation with Netherlands over West Papua (1961-1962)
- Opposition to Malaysia (Konfrontasi, 1963-1966)
- Rising military influence

### New Order under Suharto (1967-1998)

**Suharto Presidency:**
- Rose to power after 1965 failed coup blamed on Communist Party (PKI)
- Mass killings of suspected communists (1965-1966): 500,000-1,000,000 killed
- "New Order" authoritarian regime
- US-backed anti-communist alignment
- Economic development and industrialization
- Corruption and cronyism (Suharto family amassed billions)

**Foreign Policy:**
- Turned from Sukarno's anti-Western stance to Western alignment
- ASEAN founding member (1967)
- Invasion of East Timor (1975)
- Ended confrontation with Malaysia
- Acceptance of foreign investment

**End of New Order:**
- 1997 Asian Financial Crisis devastated Indonesian economy
- Mass protests and riots
- Suharto resigned May 21, 1998

### Reformasi Era (1998-Present)

**Democratic Transition:**
- Constitutional reforms
- Direct presidential elections (since 2004)
- Press freedom expanded
- Decentralization to regions
- Separation of police from military (1999)

**Key Events:**
- East Timor independence referendum (1999)
- Multiple terrorist attacks (2002-2009 Bali bombings)
- 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami (170,000+ killed in Aceh)
- Aceh peace agreement (2005)
- Successful democratic transitions

**Presidents:**
- B.J. Habibie (1998-1999)
- Abdurrahman Wahid (1999-2001)
- Megawati Sukarnoputri (2001-2004)
- Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono (2004-2014)
- Joko Widodo (2014-2024)
- Prabowo Subianto (2024-present)
