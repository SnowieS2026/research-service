- Cold War pivot: US-Spain Pact of Madrid (1953) - bases for aid
- Economic liberalization: 1959 Stabilization Plan
- Growth: "Spanish Miracle" (1959-1974) - 7% annual growth
- Late Franco repression: Limited political opening

**Key Events:**
- 1947: Law of Succession (monarchy restoration plan)

- 1955: UN membership
- 1959: Stabilization Plan (IMF/World Bank)
- 1969: Juan Carlos designated successor
- 1973: Carrero Blanco assassination (ETA)
- 1975: Franco death, Juan Carlos I becomes king

### Democratic Transition (1975-1986)

**Transition to Democracy:**
- 1975: King Juan Carlos I assumes throne
- 1976: Adolfo Suárez appointed PM, Political Reform Law
- 1977: First democratic elections, Suárez (UCD) wins
- 1978: Democratic Constitution approved (88% referendum)
- 1979: Basque and Catalan autonomy statutes
- 1981: 23-F coup attempt (defeated by King)
- 1982: PSOE victory (Felipe González) - 14 years in power

**Key Accomplishments:**
- Peaceful transition (lauded internationally)
- Autonomy system for regions (asymmetrical federalism)
- NATO membership referendum (1986, narrowly approved)
- EU membership negotiations (joined 1986)
