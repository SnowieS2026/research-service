## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### South Korea and the North Korea-Russia Alliance — Security Crisis (March 2026)
- **South Korea deeply alarmed by the North Korea-Russia defence treaty** — Pyongyang now sharing technology with Moscow.
- **Kim Jong-un receiving advanced submarine and missile technology** from Russia — a direct threat to South Korea's security.
- South Korea has **warned it may supply lethal weapons to Ukraine** — in direct response to North Korean troops fighting for Russia.

### North Korea's ICBM Threat — Directly Targeting South Korea and Japan (March 2026)
- **North Korea tested the Hwasong-18 solid-fuel ICBM** — capable of striking the US mainland, but also optimised for regional targets.
- South Korea's **THAAD missile defence system** — deployed in Seongju, capable of intercepting North Korean missiles.
- South Korea developing **Kill Chain** pre-emptive strike system — to destroy North Korean missiles before launch.

## 🇺🇸 US-South Korea Alliance — Rock Solid (March 2026)

### US Forces Korea — 28,500 Troops (March 2026)
- **~28,500 US troops stationed in South Korea** — combined US-ROK military command.
- **Atom sharing discussions** — South Korea increasingly discussing whether it should develop its own nuclear weapons given North Korea's advancing programme.
- South Korea **has ayeonhas** — US nuclear umbrella protects South Korea; US tactical nuclear weapons deployed in South Korea (B83 gravity bombs).
- **Integrated deterrence** — US-ROK conducting exercises simulating joint nuclear operations.

### Samsung and SK Hynix — The Chip Alliance Under Pressure (March 2026)
- **Samsung and SK Hynix** — South Korea's semiconductor giants — dominate global DRAM and NAND flash memory.
- US **CHIPS Act subsidies flowing to Samsung** — Samsung building a $17B chip factory in Texas.
- South Korea navigating the **US-China tech war** — can't sell advanced chips to China (US restrictions) but China is a huge market.
- China accounts for ~40% of Samsung's and SK Hynix's revenue — a difficult position.

## 🏛️ Domestic Politics — Yoon's Scandal and Recovery (March 2026)

### President Yoon Suk-yeol — martial law crisis aftermath (March 2026)
- **Yoon's December 2024 martial law declaration** caused massive political fallout — impeachment passed by parliament.
- Yoon removed from office — **President Han Duck-soo** serving as acting president/minister.
- Fresh elections — South Korea navigating constitutional succession crisis.
- The martial law episode revealed **deep polarisation** in South Korean society — progressive vs conservative, urban vs rural.

### South Korea's Political Cycle — 2027 Presidential Election (March 2026)
- The main candidates will likely be from the **Democratic Party (progressive)** and the **People Power Party (conservative)**.
- Key issues: North Korea threat, economy (K-pop, chips, shipping), and relations with China.

## 🛡️ Military Capabilities — Facing North Korea Alone (March 2026)

### South Korea's World-Class Military (March 2026)
- **675,000 active duty troops** — one of the largest militaries in the world by active personnel.
- **KF-21 Boramae** — indigenous 4.5 generation fighter, one of few countries developing their own advanced aircraft.
- **KSS-III submarines** — advanced diesel-electric subs with ballistic missile capabilities.
- **Hyunmoo missiles** — ballistic and cruise missiles capable of striking all of North Korea.
- South Korea has **massive conventional superiority over North Korea** — but North Korea's nuclear weapons negates this advantage.
