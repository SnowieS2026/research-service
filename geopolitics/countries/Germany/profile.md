## 7. Historical Context

### Post-WWII Reconstruction (1945–1955)
- **Division:** Occupied by Allies; Federal Republic (West) and GDR (East) established 1949
- **Economic miracle (Wirtschaftswunder):** 1950s rapid industrialization under Ludwig Erhard
- **Sovereignty:** Full sovereignty restored 1955; NATO membership same year

### Cold War Era (1955–1990)
- **Frontline state:** NATO vs Warsaw Pact — largest conventional force in Europe
- **Ostpolitik (1969–1974):** Willy Brandt's policy of engagement with Eastern Bloc
- **Nuclear controversy:** 1980s peace movement against Pershing II deployment
- **Economic rise:** 1970s–80s — emerged as economic superpower

### Reunification (1989–1991)
- **Fall of Berlin Wall:** November 9, 1989
- **Reunification:** October 3, 1990
- **Cost:** €2+ trillion in transfers from West to East (ongoing)
- **Integration challenges:** Economic gap narrowed but persists — East Germany ~85% of West GDP per capita

### Post-Reunification (1991–2022)
- **EU deepening:** Maastricht Treaty (1992), Euro adoption (1999)
- **EU expansion:** Champion of Eastern enlargement (2004, 2007)
- **Military restraint:** Post-WWII culture of military caution; limited participation in Kosovo (1999), Afghanistan (2001–2021)
- **Merkel era (2005–2021):** 16 years of stability; Euro crisis management; refugee crisis (2015); Nord Stream 2 approval

### Zeitenwende (2022–Present)
- **February 27, 2022:** Chancellor Scholz's historic speech declaring paradigm shift
- **Key commitments:** €100B special fund; 2% GDP defense; energy decoupling from Russia; Ukraine support
- **End of Ostpolitik:** Russia policy fundamentally reset
- **Merz continuation (2025):** Accelerated defense spending, Taurus missiles to Ukraine, China de-risking
