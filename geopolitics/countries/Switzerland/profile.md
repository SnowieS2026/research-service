## 2. Leadership & Politics

### Federal Council (Bundesrat) — 7-Member Collegial Executive

The Swiss Federal Council is unique among democracies: a seven-member collegial executive with no single head of state. The presidency rotates annually among members. Collective decision-making is binding.

**Current Federal Council (2025):**

| Councilor | Party | Portfolio | Since |

*Note: Cabinet composition may shift following 2023 federal elections.*

### Magic Formula (Zauberformel)

Since 1959, the Federal Council has maintained an informal "magic formula" for proportional party representation:
- **SVP (Swiss People's Party):** 2 seats
- **FDP (Liberals):** 2 seats
- **SP (Social Democrats):** 2 seats
- **CVP/PDC (Center Party):** 1 seat (now called "The Center")

This consensus model ensures broad political stability and prevents polarization.

### Parliament (Federal Assembly)

- **National Council (Nationalrat):** 200 seats, proportional representation
- **Council of States (Ständerat):** 46 seats, two per canton (one per half-canton)
- Elections every 4 years; referendums held quarterly

### Unique Democratic Features

**Direct Democracy:**
- 100,000 signatures triggers constitutional amendment referendum
- 50,000 signatures triggers legislative referendum
- ~4-5 national referendums per year
- High voter participation in direct democracy (40-50% average)

**Cantonal Sovereignty:**
- 26 cantons retain significant autonomy (taxation, education, police)
- Cantons control ~40% of public spending
- Each canton has its own constitution and courts

## 3. Foreign Relations

### Core Principle: Armed Neutrality

Switzerland's defining geopolitical stance is **permanent neutrality**, declared in 1815 and recognized internationally at the Congress of Vienna. This is not passive neutrality but **armed neutrality** — Switzerland maintains substantial defense capabilities and universal conscription.

### European Union Relations

Switzerland is **not an EU member** but deeply integrated:
- **Bilateral Agreements I (1999/2002):** 120+ treaties covering free movement, trade, research
- **Bilateral Agreements II (2004):** Schengen/Dublin association, taxation
- **EU-Swiss Framework:** Ongoing negotiations for institutional framework
- **Key Friction Points:**
  - Immigration quotas (SVP initiative 2014 caps EU citizens)
  - Banking transparency (automatic info exchange adopted 2017)
  - Financial sector access

**Current Status (2025):** Negotiations continue on framework agreement to replace bilateral patchwork. Switzerland pays into EU cohesion funds (~CHF 1.1 billion) in exchange for market access.

### United States Relations

Historically close ties despite occasional friction:
- **WWII Controversy:** Gold purchases from Nazi Germany, Jewish asset issues
- **Banking Secrecy:** UBS and Credit Suisse settlements (2009, 2018) ended traditional secrecy
- **Trade:** U.S. is Switzerland's #2 export destination (CHF 52B, 2024)
- **Defense:** Purchases U.S. military equipment (F-35s, Patriot missiles)
- **Tech/Pharma:** Strong investment corridor (Novartis, Roche, Google Zürich)

### Russia & Ukraine Position (2022-2025)

Switzerland broke with strict neutrality precedent:
- **EU Sanctions Adoption:** Adopted all EU sanctions packages against Russia (unprecedented for neutral state)
- **Asset Freezes:** ~CHF 7.5 billion in Russian assets frozen (2024)
- **Humanitarian Role:** Geneva hosts Ukraine peace talks (2022, ongoing)
- **ICRC Base:** International Committee of the Red Cross headquarters in Geneva
- **Contentious:** Some EU officials criticize Switzerland for not allowing re-export of Swiss weapons to Ukraine

### Key Diplomatic Roles

**Geneva Hub:** Host to 42 international organizations:
- UN European headquarters
- WTO, WHO, ILO, WIPO, ITU
- International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC)
- World Economic Forum (Davos)

**Mediation Role:**
- Hosted U.S.-Iran nuclear talks (P5+1)
- U.S.-Cuba diplomatic channel
- North Korean interests section for U.S.
- Numerous conflict resolution initiatives

## 4. Military Capabilities

### Neutrality Does Not Mean Disarmament

Switzerland maintains one of Europe's best-equipped militaries per capita, rooted in the militia system.

**Forces (2025):**

| Branch | Personnel | Equipment |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Army** | 100,000 active (militia) | Leopard 2 tanks, M109 howitzers |
| **Air Force** | 21,000 | F/A-18 (30), F-35A on order (36) |
| **Navy** | None (landlocked) | — |

**Total Mobilizable Strength:** ~140,000 (including reserves)

### Conscription System

- **Mandatory Service:** All male citizens serve at age 19 (18-21 weeks basic)
- **Alternative Civilian Service:** Available for conscientious objectors (1.5× longer)
- **Reserve Obligations:** Until age 34 (officers: 50)
- **Women:** Voluntary service since 1995
- **Annual Training:** 3-4 weeks per year through age 30

### Strategic Assets

**Civil Defense (Swiss Unique):**
- **Nuclear Shelters:** 360,000+ shelters, capacity for entire population
- **Mandatory Construction:** All buildings since 1963 must include shelter
- **Stockpiling:** 6 months food reserves, fuel reserves, medicine
- **Total War Infrastructure:** Bridges, tunnels pre-rigged for demolition

**Air Defense:**
- Patriot and SAMP/T systems
- Ground-based air defense modernization (BODLUV program)
- Air policing 24/7 since 2022 (previously limited hours)

**Recent Procurements:**
- **F-35A Lightning II:** CHF 6 billion, 36 aircraft (delivery 2027-2030)
- **Patriot Systems:** Upgrade and expansion (CHF 2.1 billion)
- **Leopard 2 Upgrade:** CHF 800 million modernization

### Defense Budget

- **Annual:** CHF 5.6 billion (~$6.2B, 2024)
- **% of GDP:** 0.7% (lowest in Europe except Iceland)
- **NATO Comparison:** Would be ~2.0% if including militia training costs

### Nuclear Weapons: None

Switzerland **does not possess nuclear weapons**. It signed the NPT in 1969 and the Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons in 2019. During the Cold War, Switzerland considered a nuclear weapons program (1950s-1960s) but abandoned it due to cost and the nuclear non-proliferation regime.

### Energy Profile

**Import Dependent:**
- **Oil:** 100% imported (~40% of energy)
- **Natural Gas:** 100% imported (Russian gas phased out post-2022, now EU/LNG)
- **Nuclear:** 30% of electricity (4 reactors, Beznau 1/2, Gösgen)
- **Hydroelectric:** 60% of electricity (1,000+ plants)
- **Renewables:** Growing solar/wind (~5%)

**Energy Security:**
- Strategic petroleum reserves: 4.5 months
- Gas storage: 2 months equivalent
- Working to increase renewable share to 50%+ by 2050

### Critical Minerals & Resources

Switzerland has **few domestic mineral resources**:
- Cement/limestone (local)
- Gravel and sand (local)
- Gold (refining hub, not mining)
- **Dependency:** Virtually all critical minerals (rare earths, lithium, cobalt) imported

**Gold Hub:** Switzerland refines 70%+ of world's gold:
- Major refineries: Valcambi, PAMP, Argor-Heraeus
- ~2,000+ tonnes refined annually
- Key hub for gold flows (London-Zurich-Hong Kong)

### Banking & Financial Sector

**Historical Significance:** Swiss banking secrecy (1934 Banking Act) made Switzerland the world's premier wealth haven.

**Post-2009 Transformation:**
- UBS settlement with U.S. DOJ (2009) ended traditional secrecy
- Automatic exchange of information (AEOI) adopted 2017
- OECD "white list" compliance
- Banking secrecy now applies only to tax residents

**Assets Under Management:**
- CHF 7.8 trillion (2024)
- ~25% of global cross-border private wealth
- Largest offshore wealth center globally

**Key Institutions:**
- UBS (merged with Credit Suisse 2023): CHF 1.6T assets
- Julius Bär: CHF 470B assets
- Pictet, Lombard Odier, Mirabaud (private banks)

## 6. Geopolitical Role

### Regional Role: Alpine Core

Switzerland anchors Central Europe as:
- **Transit Hub:** Gotthard Base Tunnel (world's longest, 57km) connects north-south Europe
- **Hydro Diplomacy:** Controls headwaters of Rhine, Rhône, Ticino (Po), Inn (Danube)
- **Border Position:** EU/EFTA member surrounded by EU (DE, FR, IT, AT, LI)

### Global Role: Neutral Broker

**Host Nation:** Geneva and Switzerland host more international organizations than any country except USA:

## 7. Historical Context

### Origins (1291-1648)

- **1291:** Federal Charter founding Swiss Confederacy (Uri, Schwyz, Unterwalden)
- **Expansion:** Added cantons through military conquest and alliance
- **Swabian War (1499):** De facto independence from Holy Roman Empire
- **Peace of Westphalia (1648):** Formal recognition of Swiss independence

### Napoleonic Era & 19th Century

- **1798:** French invasion, Helvetic Republic imposed
- **1803:** Act of Mediation restored cantonal autonomy
- **1815:** Congress of Vienna recognized perpetual neutrality
- **1848:** Federal Constitution created modern Swiss state (civil war resolved)
- **1863:** ICRC founded in Geneva → Geneva Conventions

### World Wars

**WWI (1914-1918):**
- Remained neutral
- Economic hardship (blockades)
- 1918 general strike (social tensions)
- Post-war: joined League of Nations (Geneva as headquarters)

**WWII (1939-1945):**
- Strict armed neutrality maintained
- Surrounded by Axis powers (Germany, Italy, occupied France)
- **Controversies:**
  - Gold transactions with Nazi Germany (CHF 400M+)
  - Refugee policy (turned away ~25,000 Jewish refugees)
  - Post-war: frozen Jewish assets controversy
- **Defense:** 850,000 troops mobilized (20% of population)
- **Diplomacy:** Protected Allied interests in Axis territories

### Cold War

- Never joined NATO
- Armed neutrality with Soviet contingency plans
- Developed comprehensive civil defense (nuclear shelters)
- Economic integration with West (EFTA 1960)
- Banking secrecy attracted global capital

### Post-Cold War

- **2002:** Joined UN (with reservations)
- **Not joined:** EU, NATO, EEA
- **2008:** Banking secrecy erosion began (UBS settlement)
- **2014:** Immigration quota initiative (tension with EU)
- **2022:** Adopted EU sanctions against Russia (unprecedented for neutral state)

## 9. Notable Ties to Other Countries

### Germany

- **Largest trade partner** (33% exports, 48% imports)
- Shared language/culture (German-speaking majority)
- Cross-border workers: ~400,000 commute daily
- Historical: complex relationship during WWII
- Current: close economic integration, differing neutrality stance

- **Second-largest trade partner**
- Shared language/culture (French-speaking cantons)
- Geneva: cross-border metropolitan area
- Diplomatic: close coordination (both EU-adjacent)

- **Third-largest trade partner**
- Shared language/culture (Italian-speaking cantons)
- Transit corridor: Gotthard, Lötschberg tunnels
- Migration: significant Italian diaspora in Switzerland

### United States

- **Major trade partner** (#2 export destination)
- Deep economic ties: Swiss companies in USA (Nestlé, Novartis, Roche)
- Financial sector: post-2009 regulatory alignment
- Defense: F-35 procurement, NATO partnership (PfP)
- Diplomatic: Switzerland represents U.S. interests in Iran, North Korea

- Post-Brexit: new bilateral agreements negotiated
