## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Iran-Israel War — Egypt Caught in the Middle (Late February–March 2026)
- **US-Israel strikes on Iran began February 28, 2026** — Egypt immediately affected as a key regional mediator and neighbour of Gaza.
- **Iran's navy chief killed by Israeli strike** (March 27, 2026) — Iran's navy chief overseeing Strait of Hormuz blockade killed. Israel said other senior navy officials also killed. Egypt's position between Israel and Iran makes it strategically exposed.
- **Gaza ceasefire negotiations:** Egypt is the primary mediator between Israel and Hamas — continuing to push for a ceasefire even as the broader regional war escalates.
- Egypt has historically been the most important Arab mediator in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict — the IDF's ongoing operations in Gaza have strained Egypt's relationship with Israel.
- **Iran's retaliation** has included strikes on Gulf neighbours — Egypt's Red Sea coast and Suez Canal route are at risk if the conflict spreads.

### Turkey–Egypt Relations Normalised — Erdogan Visits Cairo (February 2026)
- **Turkish President Erdogan visited Cairo in February 2026** — the first such visit in years, marking the full normalisation of Turkish-Egyptian relations.
- The two countries, both major regional powers with competing interests in Libya, the Eastern Mediterranean, and Gaza, had been at odds since 2013 when Egypt's military removed the Muslim Brotherhood government.
- This rapprochement is significant for **regional power dynamics** — Turkey and Egypt together hold considerable influence over the Arab world and Mediterranean.
- A **Trilateral Mediation Mechanism** (Turkey-Egypt-Qatar) was announced to coordinate on Gaza — a rare instance of Turkish-Egyptian cooperation.

### Sudan War — Egyptian Involvement and Regional Spillover
- The **civil war in Sudan** (between the SAF and RSF) continues to rage — Egypt has been providing support to the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) while the UAE backs the RSF.
- **Egyptian troops and border security** are a concern as the conflict threatens to spill across Egypt's southern border.
- Egypt hosting Sudanese refugees — the humanitarian crisis deepening.
- Egypt's strategic interest: a stable Sudan is critical for Egypt's water security (Nile) and regional influence.

## 💧 GERD Crisis — Ethiopia Fills Dam Without Agreement (October 2024 Ongoing)

- **Ethiopia completed the fourth filling of the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD) in October 2024** without reaching a binding agreement with Egypt and Sudan.
- This represents a **critical existential threat to Egypt** — Egypt depends on the Nile for 97% of its freshwater supply.
- **Egypt has demanded binding arbitration** at the African Union and UN Security Council but Ethiopia has refused.
- The situation remains at an impasse — Egypt continuing to push for an international legal framework but with limited leverage.
- This is arguably **Egypt's most existential threat** — more serious than Gaza in the long term, yet receiving less international attention.

## 🇪🇬 Domestic Politics

### El-Sisi — Third Term Under Pressure
- President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi continues to dominate Egyptian politics — third term running until 2030.
- **Economic challenges mounting** — Egypt's currency has depreciated sharply, inflation remains high, and external debt ($155B) is a significant burden.
- IMF program ongoing — Cairo implementing reforms in exchange for the $8B IMF loan facility.
- **Military controls 30-40% of the economy** — continuing to entrench military business interests.

### Israel-Egypt Peace Treaty Under Strain
- The 1979 Camp David Accords peace treaty between Egypt and Israel is under unprecedented strain.
- Egypt has repeatedly warned Israel that its military operations in Gaza must stop — though Egypt has not withdrawn its ambassador.
- The treaty remains in place but is politically toxic domestically — Egyptians increasingly critical of normalisation.

## 🛢️ Economy & Suez Canal

- **Suez Canal revenue under pressure** — shipping disruptions from Red Sea Houthi attacks have affected canal traffic, though tensions with Iran could redirect some traffic back through the canal.
- **External debt crisis** — $155B in external debt; servicing costs are significant.
- **Remittances from Egyptians abroad** — $29.5B annually, a crucial source of foreign currency.
- **Tourism** — a key sector slowly recovering; the Iran conflict has the potential to further suppress regional tourism.

## 🛡️ Military Capabilities

- **Largest conventional military in the Arab world** (438,500 active) — Egypt spending heavily on defence.
- **2nd largest F-16 fleet outside the US/Israel** — 220+ aircraft; major US military aid recipient.
- **4,600+ tanks** (primarily M1A1 Abrams sourced from the US).
- Nuclear-capable but maintains a civilian nuclear program under NPT — the Dabaa nuclear power plant being built with Russian assistance.
- **US military aid ($1.3B annually)** — Egypt is the largest recipient of US military assistance; Congress has periodically conditioned this on human rights improvements.
