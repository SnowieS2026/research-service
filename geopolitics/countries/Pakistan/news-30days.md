## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Pakistan's Economic Crisis — IMF Programme at Risk (March 2026)
- **Pakistan is in a severe economic crisis** — external debt ~$130B, foreign reserves critically low.
- **IMF programme ongoing** — Pakistan barely meeting conditions, with bailouts from Saudi Arabia, UAE, and China keeping the country solvent.
- Pakistan's **external debt servicing is crushing** — every dollar of foreign exchange earned goes to debt repayment.
- **Currency (rupee) weak** — import-dependent economy suffering from dollar scarcity.
- **Inflation remains high** — ordinary Pakistanis experiencing a cost-of-living crisis.

### Pakistan–India Ceasefire — Fragile but Holding (April 2025 Onward)
- **Pakistan and India agreed to a ceasefire** following the most serious military crisis between the two nuclear-armed neighbours in decades.
- The crisis saw **direct military exchanges** — Indian and Pakistani forces clashed across the Line of Control.
- The ceasefire, brokered with US/UK/Saudi involvement, is still in place — but tensions remain extremely high.
- **Kashmir remains the core dispute** — both countries claim it in full; a UN-mediated resolution remains elusive.

## 🇵🇰 Pakistan's Political Crisis — Imran Khan and the Military (March 2026)

### Imran Khan — Imprisoned but Politically Alive (March 2026)
- **Former Prime Minister Imran Khan remains imprisoned** — convicted on multiple charges his supporters say are politically motivated.
- PTI (Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf) — Imran's party — banned from elections in 2024, forcing it underground.
- **Army Chief General Asim Munir** is the most powerful figure in Pakistan — civilian governments rise and fall but the military pulls the strings on security and foreign policy.
- The military has **increasingly suppressed dissent** — media freedom restricted, civil society organisations targeted.

## 🇹🇷 Afghanistan and the TTP — Pakistan's Western Border Crisis (March 2026)

### Taliban-Controlled Afghanistan — Pakistan's Problem (March 2026)
- **Afghanistan remains under Taliban control** — Pakistan's military sees Afghan Taliban (TTP) as a major threat.
- TTP (Tehreek-e-Taliban Pakistan) — an umbrella group of Pakistani Taliban — launching attacks from Afghan soil into Pakistan.
- **Pakistan has carried out cross-border strikes into Afghanistan** — targeting TTP camps, angering the Taliban government.
- The Taliban government is unlikely to expel the TTP — using it as leverage against Pakistan.

## 🇨🇳 China–Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC) — $62 Billion at Risk (March 2026)

### CPEC Phase II — Slow Progress (March 2026)
- **CPEC** — China's $62B investment in Pakistani infrastructure — is in its second phase.
- Phase I focused on energy and transport infrastructure; **Phase II is supposed to focus on industrial cooperation and agriculture**.
- Pakistan's economic crisis has **slowed CPEC implementation** — Chinese companies are demanding better returns and guarantees.
- Pakistan is **deeply indebted to China** — CPEC loans are a growing concern for Pakistan's fiscal position.

## 🇮🇷 Iran and Pakistan — Shia-Sunni Tensions (March 2026)

### Pakistan–Iran Border Tensions (March 2026)
- **Pakistan and Iran have had border skirmishes** — Iranian Revolutionary Guard operating in Pakistan's Balochistan province.
- Both countries support different militant proxies — Iran supporting Shia groups, Pakistan supporting Sunni militants in Afghanistan and Iran.
- **The Iran-Israel war has complicated Pakistan's position** — Pakistan has historical ties to Sunni militant groups that Iran views as threats.

### CPEC Energy Projects — Reducing Load Shedding (March 2026)
- CPEC Phase I built **coal and hydroelectric power plants** — reducing Pakistan's chronic electricity shortages.
- However, Pakistan still faces **energy shortfalls** — particularly in summer when demand surges.
- Pakistan exploring **LNG imports** from Qatar and other suppliers — to meet peak demand.
