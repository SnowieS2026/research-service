## 2. Leadership & Politics

### Current Government (2025-2026)

**Head of State:** President Asif Ali Zardari (PPP) — ceremonial role, elected March 2024

**Head of Government:** Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif (PML-N) — assumed office March 2024 after contentious elections

**Key Cabinet Positions:**
- Foreign Minister: Ishaq Dar (PML-N)
- Finance Minister: Muhammad Aurangzeb (technocrat)
- Defense Minister: Khawaja Muhammad Asif (PML-N)
- Interior Minister: Mohsin Naqvi (independent, military-aligned)

### Political Landscape

**Pakistan Muslim League-Nawaz (PML-N):**
- Center-right party, stronghold in Punjab
- Led by Sharif family dynasty (Nawaz Sharif, Shehbaz Sharif)
- Pro-business, historically close to Saudi Arabia and China
- Currently in governing coalition

**Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP):**
- Center-left, stronghold in Sindh
- Led by Bhutto-Zardari family (Bilawal Bhutto Zardari)
- Secular orientation, founded by Zulfikar Ali Bhutto
- Coalition partner in government

**Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI):**
- Center-right/nationalist party founded by Imran Khan
- Strongest in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa
- Currently facing severe repression: Imran Khan imprisoned since August 2023
- Mass mobilization capacity but heavily constrained by state apparatus

**Religious Parties:**
- Jamaat-e-Islami, Jamiat Ulema-e-Islam (JUI-F) — Islamist parties with electoral blocs
- Limited electoral success but influence on social policy

### The Military Establishment (Pakistan Army)

**Chief of Army Staff (COAS):** General Asim Munir (since November 2022)
- First ISI chief to become army chief
- Oversaw Pakistan's nuclear program as Director General Strategic Plans Division
- Led May 2025 response to India crisis

**Inter-Services Intelligence (ISI):**
- Director General: Lt. Gen. Nadeem Anjum (as of 2025)
- Functions: External intelligence, domestic surveillance, political mediation
- Estimated 25,000+ personnel with vast networks
- Historically orchestrates political outcomes, supports militant proxies

**Military Influence:**
- Three major coups: Ayub Khan (1958), Zia-ul-Haq (1977), Pervez Musharraf (1999)
- Current model: "Hybrid regime" — civilian facade with military supremacy
- Controls nuclear arsenal, defense budget (~17% of government spending), and foreign policy toward India/Afghanistan
- Vast economic empire: Fauji Foundation, Army Welfare Trust, Bahria Town real estate

**Judiciary:**
- Supreme Court has been weaponized against opposition (PTI)
- Chief Justice Qazi Faez Isa (2025) has attempted reforms but limited success

### Political Crisis & Governance Challenges

**2024 Elections Controversy:**
- PTI candidates forced to run as independents after party banned from using its symbol
- Allegations of military interference, vote rigging, and pre-poll engineering
- Low turnout (~47%), widespread protests

**Democratic Erosion:**
- Freedom House rating: "Partly Free" (score 27/100)
- Press censorship, journalist arrests, social media restrictions (Twitter/X blocked periodically)
- Sedition laws used against critics

**Internal Security:**
- Terrorism resurgence: TTP (Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan), Baluch separatists, ISIS-K
- 2025 saw deadliest attacks since 2018 (~800+ civilian deaths)
- Tensions with Afghanistan over militant sanctuaries

## 3. Foreign Relations

### Strategic Doctrine

Pakistan's foreign policy centers on:
1. **India containment** — existential threat perception drives defense posture
2. **China alliance** — "all-weather friend," counterbalance to India
3. **United States management** — transactional relationship for aid/security
4. **Gulf engagement** — economic lifeline (remittances, energy, labor export)
5. **Afghanistan influence** — strategic depth doctrine (increasingly contested)

### Key Relationships

#### **China — Ironclad Ally**

**Nature:** "Higher than mountains, deeper than oceans, sweeter than honey"

**Economic:**
- China-Pakistan Economic Corridor (CPEC): $62+ billion infrastructure investment
- Port of Gwadar: Deep-water port, gateway to Arabian Sea, Chinese-operated
- Energy projects: Coal, hydro, solar plants totaling 17,000 MW
- Debt dependency: Pakistan owes China ~$30 billion (25%+ of external debt)

**Military:**
- Arms supplier: JF-17 Thunder fighter (joint production), J-10C jets, Type 054AP frigates
- Naval cooperation: Chinese-built submarines, naval base access discussions
- Border: China-Pakistan border agreement, Karakoram Highway, Khunjerab Pass
- Intelligence sharing and counterterrorism coordination

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Both view India as rival
- Belt and Road Initiative flagship
- China's access to Arabian Sea bypassing Strait of Malacca
- Nuclear cooperation (historical — China allegedly supported Pakistan's program)

**Tensions:**
- Slow CPEC progress, debt distress
- Baluch insurgency targeting Chinese workers
- Xinjiang concerns — Pakistan's Uighur position

#### **United States — Transactional Partnership**

**Historical Context:**
- Cold War ally against Soviet Union (SEATO, CENTO)
- Anti-Soviet jihad financier (1979-1989): $6+ billion in aid
- Post-9/11 "major non-NATO ally" for War on Terror: $33+ billion since 2002
- 2010s: Relations deteriorated over Pakistan's Taliban sanctuaries
- 2018: US suspended security aid ($300M+)

**2025 Reset:**
- Trump 2.0 administration opens new engagement
- Pakistan nominates Trump for Nobel Peace Prize (India crisis mediation)
- US sees Pakistan as: (1) Afghanistan stabilizer, (2) Iran pressure lever, (3) mineral partner (rare earths)
- Counterterrorism cooperation resuming (intelligence sharing on ISIS-K/TTP)
- No major arms sales since 2018 F-16 dispute

**Friction Points:**
- Pakistan's China alignment
- Democracy/human rights concerns
- Nuclear proliferation history (A.Q. Khan network)
- Afghanistan Taliban ties

#### **India — Existential Rival**

**Nature:** Hostile, nuclear-armed neighbors with 4 wars (1947-48, 1965, 1971, 1999)

**Kashmir:**
- Core dispute: Both claim entire Kashmir region
- 2019: India revoked Article 370 (Kashmir autonomy), Pakistan protested
- Line of Control (LoC): Active military zone, frequent skirmishes
- Pakistan supports Kashmiri separatists (militant proxies historically)

**May 2025 Crisis:**
- Triggered by alleged terrorist attack in Indian-administered Kashmir
- India conducted surgical strikes across LoC
- Pakistan responded with air operations
- Nuclear signals communicated through back channels
- Ceasefire brokered with US/Chinese diplomatic intervention
- Both sides claimed victory; status quo restored

**Nuclear Doctrine:**
- Pakistan maintains first-use doctrine vs. India
- Tactical nuclear weapons (Nasr/Hatf-9) for battlefield use
- India: No-first-use doctrine (though increasingly ambiguous)

**Current Posture:**
- No formal dialogue since 2019
- Track-II diplomacy continues
- Trade minimal (suspended since 2019)
- Cultural/sporting exchanges frozen

#### **Afghanistan — Complicated Dependency**

**Historical Role:**
- Pakistan's ISI instrumental in Taliban's rise (1994-2001, 2021)
- "Strategic depth" doctrine: Afghanistan as rear area vs. India
- Hosted 3+ million Afghan refugees since 1979

**Post-2021 (Taliban Return):**
- Initially celebrated as victory; reality proved complex
- TTP (Pakistani Taliban) resurged from Afghan sanctuaries
- Border clashes increased
- Diplomatic tensions over fence construction (Durand Line disputes)
- 2024-2025: Relations deteriorated sharply

**Current Tension:**
- Afghanistan Taliban won't act against TTP (ideological kin)
- Pakistan expelled 500,000+ Afghan refugees (2023-2025)
- Trade disruptions at Torkham border
- Pakistan seeks Chinese/Russian mediation

#### **Saudi Arabia & Gulf States — Economic Lifeline**

**Saudi Arabia:**
- Defense pact signed 2025 (mutual defense cooperation)
- $3+ billion in deposits/loans
- Pakistani military presence: Training missions, protection of holy sites
- Labor: 2+ million Pakistanis in Saudi Arabia, $6-8 billion annual remittances
- Islamic diplomacy: Pakistan backs Saudi positions (Yemen, Qatar crisis)

**United Arab Emirates:**
- Major investor: Dubai-based holdings, real estate
- $3+ billion bailout packages (2023-2024)
- Labor remittances: $3 billion/year
- Port interests: Investment in Karachi Port

**Qatar:**
- LNG supplier to Pakistan
- Investment commitments $3+ billion
- Labor remittances significant

**Iran:**
- Complex relationship: Cooperation (border management, gas pipeline project) + competition (Gulf rivalry)
- Pakistan walks tightrope between Iran and Saudi Arabia
- Gas pipeline (IP Gas) stalled due to US sanctions pressure
- Border security cooperation against Baluch insurgents (both sides)

#### **Russia — Growing Engagement**

**Historical:** Cold War adversary (Soviet-Afghan War, Pakistan backed mujahideen)

**Recent Shift:**
- Arms purchases: Mi-35 helicopters, discussions on air defense systems
- Energy: Pakistan Stream gas pipeline (formerly North-South pipeline)
- Afghanistan: Shared interest in Taliban engagement
- Military exercises: Joint drills since 2016
- Russia sees Pakistan as China ally and India-diversification

#### **Turkey — Brotherly Relations**

**Cultural/Islamic Bonds:**
- "Two nations, one heart" rhetoric
- Defense cooperation: Turkey supplies drones (Bayraktar TB2), helicopters
- Pakistan supports Turkey on Cyprus, Kashmir
- Turkey backs Pakistan on Kashmir at international forums
- Cultural exchanges, media co-production

### Partition & Early Years (1947-1958)

**Partition:**
- Radcliffe Line drew arbitrary borders
- 10-14 million displaced
- 1-2 million died in communal violence
- Mass migration, communal trauma

**Founders:**
- Muhammad Ali Jinnah (Governor-General, died 1948)
- Liaquat Ali Khan (PM, assassinated 1951)
- Kashmir dispute triggered first India-Pakistan war (1947-48)

**Early Challenges:**
- Weak institutions
- Refugee crisis
- Economic dislocation
- 1956: First constitution (Islamic Republic)

- 1958: First coup, martial law
- Economic growth ("Decade of Development")
- 1965 War with India (over Kashmir, stalemate)
- Growing East-West Pakistan tensions
