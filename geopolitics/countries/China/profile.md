## 3. Foreign Relations

### United States — Primary Strategic Rival

**Status:** Confrontational competition; "most consequential bilateral relationship"

**Core Frictions:**
- Taiwan sovereignty (US "One China" policy vs. PRC claims)
- Technology controls (chip bans, AI restrictions, entity lists)
- Trade war (tariffs ongoing since 2018)
- South China Sea (freedom of navigation operations)
- Human rights (Xinjiang, Hong Kong, Tibet)
- Cyber espionage allegations

**2025 Dynamics:**
- Trump 2.0 administration: escalated tariffs, tech decoupling
- Military-to-military communication restored (late 2024)
- "De-risking" vs. "decoupling" debate continues
- Taiwan remains flashpoint; US arms sales continue
- Competition for Global South influence

**Key Framework:** "Three Wars" — public opinion, psychological, legal warfare

### Taiwan — Core Sovereignty Issue

**PRC Position:** Province of China; "reunification" inevitable
**Taiwan Position:** De facto independent (ROC); sovereignty disputed

**Key Facts:**
- 23 million population; democratic government
- Semiconductor leadership (TSMC: 90%+ advanced chips)
- US commitment: "strategic ambiguity" (Taiwan Relations Act)
- PRC options: coercion, blockade, invasion
- 2025: increased PRC military pressure; gray-zone warfare
- PRC "red line": formal Taiwan independence declaration

**US Policy:**
- Arms sales under Taiwan Relations Act
- Unofficial relations (American Institute in Taiwan)
- Strategic ambiguity on defense commitment
- Bipartisan support in Congress

### Hong Kong — Post-2019 Transformation

**Status:** Under full PRC control since National Security Law (2020)

**Key Events:**
- 2019: Mass pro-democracy protests; extradition bill trigger
- 2020: National Security Law imposed; crackdown intensifies
- 2021: Electoral overhaul; "patriots only" governance
- 2024: Article 23 additional security legislation

**Current State:**
- Autonomy effectively ended; "One Country, Two Systems" hollowed
- Opposition figures jailed or exiled
- Press freedom collapsed (Apple Daily, Stand News closed)
- Financial hub status eroding but resilient
- US response: sanctions, Hong Kong Policy Act suspended

- PRC entered war; fought US/UN forces to stalemate
- ~400,000 Chinese casualties
- Established China as military power

**Sino-Indian War (1962):**
- Border conflict; China won decisively
- Captured Aksai Chin; withdrew from other gains
- Ongoing border disputes remain

**Sino-Vietnamese War (1979):**
- Brief war; Chinese invasion punished Vietnam for Cambodia
- ~30,000 casualties combined
- Drew lessons on military weaknesses

- Territorial claims vs. multiple nations
- Island-building since 2014
- 2016 PCA ruling rejected by China

2. **1989 Tiananmen:** Isolated China; slowed reforms; party prioritized stability above all
3. **2001 WTO:** Accelerated growth; China became "world's factory"
4. **2012 Xi Era:** End of collective leadership; centralization; assertive foreign policy

## 8. Current Priorities & Strategy 2025-2026

### Domestic Priorities

**1. Economic Stabilization**
- Manage property crisis fallout
- Address local government debt
- Maintain ~5% growth target
- Stimulate consumption over investment

**2. Technology Self-Sufficiency**
- "Made in China 2025" target sectors: semiconductors, AI, EVs, robotics
- Replace Western tech dependencies
- Domestic chip production (SMIC, YMTC) despite sanctions
- AI development as national priority

**3. Demographic Crisis**
- Three-child policy (2021+); limited success
- Incentives for childbirth; childcare support
- Raising retirement age (announced 2024)
- Elderly care infrastructure

**4. Political Stability**
- Continue anti-corruption purges
- Ideological tightening (Xi Jinping Thought)
- Control over private sector (tech crackdown aftermath)
- Security apparatus expansion

### Foreign Policy Priorities

**1. Taiwan Deterrence/Coercion**
- Military pressure continues (air incursions, naval exercises)
- Economic coercion (trade restrictions, tourism bans)
- Political warfare (unification propaganda, influence operations)
- Prevent formal independence declaration

**2. US Competition Management**
- Avoid direct conflict; manage competition
- Counter US encirclement (alliances, AUKUS, QUAD)
- Seek economic leverage (rare earths, supply chains)
- Diplomatic engagement where beneficial

**3. Global South Leadership**
- BRI refinement (quality over quantity)
- BRICS expansion (new members 2024+)
- Alternative institutions (AIIB, SCO)
- "Community of common destiny" narrative

**4. Regional Dominance**
- South China Sea control consolidation
- ASEAN economic integration
- Counter Japan-India alignment
- Korea Peninsula influence (vs. US, vs. Russia-DPRK ties)

### Strategic Concept: "Chinese Dream"

Xi's "National Rejuvenation" by 2049 (PRC centennial):
- Economically: Advanced economy, high-income status
- Militarily: "World-class military" (second to none)
- Technologically: Global leader in critical technologies
