## 🌍 Iran War — China's Position

### China-Iran Strategic Partnership Deepening (March 2026)
- **China is Iran's largest trading partner** and has been providing economic and diplomatic cover for Iran during the Israel-US conflict.
- **China has refused to condemn** the US-Israel strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities.
- China positioned to **profit from the chaos** — filling trade gaps left by Western sanctions on Iran.
- **Strait of Hormuz crisis** — Iran threatening to block the Strait of Hormuz (carrying 20% of world oil/LNG). China, as the world's largest oil importer, is severely exposed.
- China reportedly urging Iran to keep the Strait open while simultaneously deepening the strategic partnership.
- Chinese companies potentially stepping in to replace Western firms exiting Iran.

### China's Middle East Strategy — "Constructive Engagement" (March 2026)
- China presenting itself as a **neutral party** in the Iran-Israel conflict while actually deepening ties with Tehran.
- Beijing leveraging the crisis to position itself as an alternative to US influence in the Middle East.

## 🏭 Economy

### Youth Unemployment — 16-24 Year Olds Hit Hardest (March 2026)
- **Youth unemployment rate for 16-24 year olds at 16%** — millions of educated young Chinese unable to find work.
- Reflects a broader economic slowdown — the property sector crisis still rippling through the economy.
- Chinese graduates increasingly turning to lower-skilled work or leaving China entirely.
- This is a significant **domestic political risk** for the CCP.

### Property Sector — Evergrande's Collapse Continues (March 2026)
- **Evergrande** — the world's most indebted property developer — still in bankruptcy proceedings.
- Chinese property sector remains a major drag on economic growth — affecting consumer confidence and local government finances.
- Beijing continuing to bail out or restructure developers but without a comprehensive solution.

### China's Global Infrastructure Push (March 2026)
- **Belt and Road Initiative** continues — Xi hosting a major Belt and Road forum in Beijing.
- Over 60 countries attended — Xi using the forum to position China as an alternative to US global leadership.
- Chinese companies signing infrastructure deals across Africa, Central Asia, and Southeast Asia.
