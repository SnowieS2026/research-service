## 🏛️ Domestic Politics — Starmer's Labour Government (March 2026)

### Keir Starmer — Britain's New Prime Minister (March 2026)
- **Labour won a thumping majority in July 2024** — Keir Starmer replacing Rishi Sunak as PM.
- Starmer's government inheriting a **budget crisis and crumbling public services** — NHS waiting lists, prisons full, defence spending under pressure.
- **Rachel Reeves (Chancellor)** — running the budget; tax rises and spending cuts to fix the fiscal black hole.
- Labour's first year: **nationalising the railways** (not yet complete), **economic turbulence**, but **stable polling**.

### Conservative Party — Post-Johnson Rebuild (March 2026)
- The **Conservatives lost badly in 2024** — now in opposition, trying to rebuild under a new leader (Rishi Sunak stepped down).
- **Kemi Badenoch** as Conservative leader — the "fiscalhawk, small-state" faction leading.
- The **Brexit hangover continues** — both major parties accepting Brexit happened but debating its terms.

- **Post-Brexit trade friction continuing** — no meaningful regulatory alignment; TCA (Trade and Cooperation Agreement) working but not beloved.
- UK still outside the **Single Market and Customs Union** — friction on services trade significant.
- **UK seeking a "Swiss-style" relationship** with the EU — EU saying no without free movement.

## 🛡️ Defence — Britain's Strategic Review (March 2026)

### Defence Spending Crisis — 2.5% NATO Target (March 2026)
- UK committed to reaching **2.5% of GDP on defence** — above NATO's 2% target but under pressure.
- UK conducting a **Strategic Defence Review (SDR)** — assessing threats from Russia, China, Iran, and terrorism.
- **Trident renewal** — the UK's nuclear deterrent (Vanguard-class submarines) being replaced with Dreadnought-class.
- **AUKUS** — UK participating in the Australia-UK-US submarine programme — nuclear-powered subs for Australia.
- UK's **aircraft carriers (Queen Elizabeth, Prince of Wales)** — operating globally; F-35Bs embarked.
