## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Netherlands Leads EU on China Tech Controls — ASML Under Pressure (March 2026)
- **The Netherlands, controlling ASML's export licences**, has become a critical node in the US-China tech war.
- **ASML** — the Dutch company that makes the world's most advanced EUV chip-making machines — is the only company that can produce extreme ultraviolet lithography machines.
- **ASML's monopoly on EUV is existential for global chip manufacturing** — TSMC, Samsung, and Intel all depend on ASML machines.
- **The Netherlands, under US pressure, has restricted Chinese access to ASML's most advanced machines** — a massive strategic decision that has significantly impacted China's semiconductor ambitions.
- China accounts for ~30% of ASML's revenue — the restrictions are costing ASML billions but serving Western strategic interests.
- **The Dutch government navigates a difficult balance** — protecting ASML's commercial interests while honouring its security commitments to the West.

### ASML Expanding in the US — A New Dynamic (March 2026)
- **ASML announcing major US expansion** — new facilities in Arizona and Texas to serve American chip fabs.
- This represents a significant shift — ASML becoming more integrated into the US semiconductor ecosystem.

## 🛡️ Defence — Netherlands as NATO Logistics Hub (March 2026)

### Netherlands Defence Spending — On Track for 2% NATO Target (March 2026)
- Netherlands committed to reaching **NATO's 2% of GDP defence spending target**.
- Hosting NATO logistics command — the **Joint Force Command (JFC)** in Brunssum is one of NATO's two strategic commands.
- **Volkel Air Base** — home to F-35s and ~10-15 US B61 nuclear bombs (part of NATO nuclear sharing).
- Netherlands contributing to NATO's **Baltic air policing**, Baltic Sea patrols, and the alliance's new force model.

## 🏦 Economy

### ASML — The Dutch Economic Crown Jewel (March 2026)
- **ASML's market capitalisation exceeds $527B** — making it Europe's most valuable technology company.
- ASML is now more valuable than **Shell, Unilever, and Philips combined** — the Dutch economy increasingly dependent on this single company.
- This creates a **strategic vulnerability** — a company of this importance to global chip supply chains will be a target for foreign interference and geopolitical pressure.

### Rotterdam Port — Europe's Largest Gateway (March 2026)
- **Rotterdam Port** handles ~469 million tonnes of cargo annually — the largest port in Europe.
- Critical hub for oil, LNG, container goods, and trade between Europe and the rest of the world.
- China's influence in Rotterdam port has been a concern — Chinese companies have stakes in European port infrastructure.
