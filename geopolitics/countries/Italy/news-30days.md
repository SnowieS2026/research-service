## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Italy's Coalition Government — Meloni Stable but Challenged (March 2026)
- PM **Giorgia Meloni** heading Italy's most stable government in years — a coalition of her Brothers of Italy (FdI), Forza Italia (FI), and Lega.
- Meloni is the longest-serving PM since Silvio Berlusconi — maintaining coalition cohesion through economic challenges and EU tensions.
- Her **foreign policy** aligning closely with NATO, EU, and the US — making Italy a reliable Western ally.
- Meloni's **relationship with the EU** has improved — she has moderated her previous eurosceptic positions.

### Italy's Defence Spending — NATO 2% Target (March 2026)
- Italy continuing to increase defence spending toward the **NATO 2% of GDP target** — currently at ~1.5% and rising.
- **GCAP (Global Combat Air Programme)** — Italy is part of the UK-Japan-Italy joint fighter programme, developing the next-generation Tempest fighter to replace the Eurofighter.
- Italy hosting **35 B61 nuclear bombs** at Aviano and Ghedi air bases — part of NATO's nuclear sharing arrangement.

## 🛡️ Libya — Italy's Key Regional Interest

### Italy's Libya Policy — Energy and Migration (March 2026)
- **Libya remains Italy's most important foreign policy priority** — Italy wants to prevent another migration crisis and secure energy supplies from Libya.
- Italy's **Eni** (energy major) is deeply invested in Libyan gas production — Libya supplies ~15% of Italy's gas.
- Italy supporting the **Libyan Government of National Unity (GNU)** in Tripoli — while maintaining back-channel relations with Haftar's LNA.
- Italy has deployed military trainers and advisors to Libya — in a limited, deniable way.

## 🇪🇺 Italy and the EU

### EU Asylum Policy — Italy's Migration Pressure (March 2026)
- Italy under pressure from **high migration flows** across the Mediterranean — arrivals from Tunisia, Libya, and Algeria.
- Meloni's government implementing stricter asylum rules — clashing with EU human rights courts.
- Italy calling for EU-wide reform of asylum rules — wanting other member states to share the burden.
- **EU-AstraZeneca vaccine dispute** resolved — Italy was part of the EU's legal action against AstraZeneca over vaccine delivery delays.

## 🇮🇹 Economy

- **Italy's debt-to-GDP ratio remains ~140%** — one of the highest in the world, limiting fiscal flexibility.
- **North-South divide** — northern Italy is one of Europe's most productive regions; southern Italy is poorer and less developed.
- **Auto industry crisis** — Italian brands (Fiat/Stellantis) facing intense competition from Chinese EVs.
- Tourism — a key sector — recovering post-pandemic but facing headwinds from Middle East conflict disrupting Mediterranean travel.

---

## 🛢️ Energy

- Italy **dependent on imported energy** — gas from Algeria, Libya, Russia (reduced), and Norway.
- **Eni** — Italy's national champion — operating globally, including in Russia's Arctic LNG projects (a controversial exception to sanctions).
- Italy helping develop **East Mediterranean gas** — Cyprus, Israel, Egypt gas pipelines to Europe running through Italy.
