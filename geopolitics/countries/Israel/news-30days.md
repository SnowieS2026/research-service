## 🔴 Breaking / Major Stories

### Israel Strikes Iranian Nuclear Facilities — Regional War Begins (February 28, 2026)
- **On February 28, 2026, Israel — with US support — launched strikes on Iranian nuclear facilities** — marking a dramatic escalation from years of shadow warfare.
- Israeli F-35 jets struck Natanz, Arak, and other nuclear sites — **setting back Iran's programme by years** but not destroying it entirely.
- **Iran retaliated** with ballistic missile strikes on Israeli military bases and Israeli territory — a significant Iranian response.
- **Iran's navy chief killed** (March 27, 2026) — Israel assassinating senior Iranian military officials in targeted strikes.
- The conflict has drawn in multiple regional actors — Lebanon, Yemen, Syria, Iraq, and the Gaza Strip are all active fronts.

### Gaza War Continues — IDF Operations Ongoing (February–March 2026)
- **The IDF's operations in Gaza** have continued through early 2026 — despite international pressure for a ceasefire.
- **Israel's stated goals:** Destroy Hamas's military capability, secure the return of all hostages, and ensure Gaza never again poses a threat.
- **Hamas remaining hostages:** Approximately 59 hostages remain in Gaza — some alive, some confirmed dead, some presumed alive.
- **Humanitarian situation in Gaza catastrophic** — UN agencies warning of famine conditions and widespread civilian casualties.
- **Ceasefire negotiations** ongoing through Qatar, Egypt, and the US — but gaps between Israeli and Hamas positions remain wide.

### Iron Swords — The Iran-Israel War (March 2026)
- Israel has officially designated the conflict with Iran **"Operation Iron Swords"** — the second time this name has been used (the first was against Hamas in October 2023).
- **Israel's strategy:** Prevent Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons by any means necessary — accepting the risk of a multi-front regional war.
- **Israel's red line:** Iran cannot be allowed to possess a nuclear weapon — Israel has acted preemptively to enforce this.

## 🛡️ Military Capabilities — Iron Dome and Beyond

### Israel's Multi-Layer Missile Defence (March 2026)
- **Iron Dome** — intercepting short-range rockets from Gaza and Lebanon (~90%+ success rate).
- **David's Sling** — medium-range interceptors.
- **Arrow 2/3** — intercepting ballistic missiles at high altitude.
- **F-35 jets** — Israel is one of the largest operators of the F-35 outside the US — used extensively in strikes on Iran.
- **Submarines** — Germany has supplied Israel with Dolphin-class submarines capable of launching nuclear-armed cruise missiles — part of Israel's "Samson option" (second-strike capability).
