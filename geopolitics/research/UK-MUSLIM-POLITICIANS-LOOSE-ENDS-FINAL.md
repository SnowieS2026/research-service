**The funding question:** No accounts filed yet (first accounts due August 2026). No disclosed donors. But the £100 initial capital and the "Global Wealth Forum" origin suggest:
- Either the company was a shell created to receive funds that couldn't be received under the original name
- Or the "GLOBAL WEALTH FORUM" branding was intended to attract investment/donations before rebranding to the political "Muslim Impact Forum" name

**PSC (Person of Significant Control) structure:**

The Kidwai family is well-established in the UK Islamic establishment. This family connection between the MIF's 75% owner and the MCB/Khan campaign is a live intelligence question that cannot be confirmed or denied from open sources. The overlap between Kidwai family positions in MCB and the MIF is significant and requires further investigation through direct inquiry or financial records.

**The Bradford address:** 203 Westgate, Bradford is a serviced office. Latitude, Nelson Street (current) is also a serviced office. No confirmed link to any Gulf state funding. But the address history and the "Global Wealth Forum" origin make the funding question more suspicious, not less.

## LOOSE END 4: THE COORDINATED NETWORK QUESTION

### Is the Khan/Yousaf/Sarwar Connection Coordinated or Coincidental?

**Evidence AGAINST coordination:**
1. Different religious backgrounds: Yousaf family = Barelvi (Sufi-influenced, traditional Sunni); Sarwar family = Deobandi (conservative Sunni, anti-Sufi). These two traditions are doctrinally opposed in some respects.
2. Different parties: SNP vs Labour vs independent
3. No documented communication between the three
4. No common financial vehicle identified

**Evidence FOR a connection:**
1. Both attended **Hutchesons' Grammar School** — this is statistically significant for two top-level Muslim politicians from the same city
2. Both families are Pakistani-Scottish business families — same social class, same ethnic origin
3. The Sarwar family and the Yousaf family may know each other through the Pakistani-Scottish community in Glasgow
4. Both Yousaf and Sarwar were supported by elements of the Glasgow Muslim community in their political rises

**Assessment:** The connection is more likely **sociological** than coordinated. Both men represent a new generation of Pakistani-Scottish Muslims who entered politics through mainstream parties. They attended the same elite school, share a community background, and rose in a context where there were very few Muslim politicians — so the same networks naturally surface both. This is elite formation, not conspiracy.

However, the **Hutchesons' alumni network** as a factor in their political access and support is plausible and worth noting.

## LOOSE END 5: MOHAMMAD SARWAR AND PAKISTANI INTELLIGENCE

### Was the Father Placed by ISI?

Mohammad Sarwar served as Governor of Punjab under BOTH PML-N (Nawaz Sharif government) and PTI (Imran Khan government) — an extraordinary position that no civilian has held under two rival political parties.

**What we know:**
- He gave up UK citizenship to take the Punjab Governor role (2013)
- He was appointed, not elected — the Governor is a ceremonial role appointed by the President on the Chief Minister's advice
- PML-N and PTI are both parties with documented Pakistani military establishment relationships, but they are political rivals

**The intelligence question:**
- Pakistani Army has historically placed its own people in key civilian positions
- Mohammad Sarwar is NOT known as an ISI asset in any documented open source
- However, no open-source investigation has definitively answered whether his appointment was purely political or had an intelligence dimension

**What this means for the investigation:** Mohammad Sarwar's position as Punjab Governor creates an **adjacent intelligence question** — not a confirmed connection. If Pakistani Army/ISI wanted a relationship with a UK Muslim politician's family, placing the father as Governor of Pakistan's most important province would be a useful long-term relationship to cultivate. But there is no evidence of this.

**Conclusion:** Mohammad Sarwar's position is notable but not evidence of an intelligence relationship. The question is live and worth revisiting if new information emerges.

However, he was a senior SNP figure during the cover-up period (2019-2021), and his silence on what he knew is notable.

**Known UK activity:**

**The connection question:** No documented evidence that Khan, Yousaf, or Sarwar had any connection to Hizb ut-Tahrir. All three have explicitly positioned themselves within mainstream parliamentary politics, not pan-Islamist politics. The Muslim Council of Britain (which has had some figures with HuT-adjacent views) is more relevant than HuT itself.

**Assessment:** No connection found. This was a lower-probability lead that did not pan out.
