# Markets tanked. Here's why.

The S&P 500 dropped 2.2% Friday as Trump's extended Iran deadline spooked investors already rattled by tariff uncertainty. Dow fell 2.17%, Nasdaq 100 slipped 2.38%. Markets were having a rough week before Friday — now they're having a rougher one.



### The Big Story

Friday's selloff wasn't random. Traders are fleeing risk assets as the US-Iran ceasefire talks drag past their deadline, with oil prices spiking on uncertainty around the Strait of Hormuz. The White House extended negotiations, but Wall Street read that as "no deal" and sold first. Tech took the hardest hit — Nasdaq's 2.38% drop suggests investors are pricing in potential energy-driven inflation that could derail the Fed's easing plans. The question everyone's asking: does this reset the tariff clock, or is this just noise before next week's inevitable headline?



### 💸 Finance

**Bitcoin's losing its hedge status.** The crypto king slipped below $69,000 Friday, nearly erasing this week's recovery. BTC now shows a 0.68 correlation with crude oil — when energy prices jump, so does Bitcoin. That's not the uncorrelated safe haven bulls promised.

**Health tech breach hits 3.4 million.** TriZetto, a major insurance processing company, confirmed hackers accessed patient data — names, Social Security numbers, insurance details — possibly since November 2024. If you've filed a health insurance claim recently, your data may already be for sale.

**Fed's steady stance isn't calming markets.** The central bank held rates at its last meeting, but that was March 18. Since then, tariff uncertainty has compounded. Traders are now pricing in a longer wait for cuts.



### 🏛️ Geopolitics

**Ransomware attack shutters Swedish grocery chain.** A cyberattack hit 200+ US companies Friday, forcing Swedish retail chain Coop to close stores nationwide. This is infrastructure-level impact — the attack exploited a widely-used software tool. Expect fallout details to emerge Monday.

**Northern Illinois University shooting.** Seven people dead after a campus attack. Details still emerging, but expect renewed debate on campus security and gun policy in the coming days.
