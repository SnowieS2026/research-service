Markets tanked 2% today. Here's why that matters.

The S&P 500 dropped 2.19%, the Dow fell 2.17%, and the Nasdaq sank 2.38% — all on a Friday. Futures were already sliding after President Trump extended the Iran negotiations deadline, but the selloff accelerated as bond yields climbed and geopolitical jitters tightened their grip. If you needed proof that markets hate uncertainty, this is it.

The Big Story

Bonds are flashing warning signs that most investors are ignoring. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed sharply today, following a +20bps rise over the past 24 hours in Australian bonds and a +12-13bps jump in Japanese JGBs. This came as the Pentagon reportedly presented options for a "final blow" to Iran involving ground forces and a massive bombing campaign — with Trump said to be considering sending an additional 10,000 troops to the Middle East. The bond market is pricing in something the equity market hasn't fully digested yet: the possibility that the Iran situation escalates from tariffs into actual war.

💸 Finance

Markets just had their worst day in weeks. The S&P 500 closed at 6,357.40 (-2.19%), the Dow at 45,104.60 (-2.17%), and the Russell 2000 at 2,443.01 (-2.49%). Trump extending the Iran deadline rattled futures early, but the continued selloff suggests deeper unease about what's coming next.

Bitcoin isn't a safe haven anymore. BTC slipped below $69,000 Friday, nearly wiping out its Monday recovery. The crypto is now tracking energy markets with a 0.68 correlation — when oil spikes on Strait of Hormuz fears, Bitcoin follows, not the other way around. The "digital gold" narrative is taking a beating.

Bond vigilantes are back. Higher yields carried through from Europe to the US session, with Asian markets selling off overnight. The 10-year yield climb is the bond market's way of saying it doesn't trust the Fed to keep rates low if inflation picks up from Middle East oil disruption.

🏛️ Geopolitics

The Pentagon wants to end Iran — permanently. Reports say military planners presented options for a "final blow" including ground forces and extensive bombing runs. This isn't posturing anymore; it's contingency planning at the highest level.

Trump may send 10,000 more troops to the Middle East. The deployment would include infantry, on top of existing forces. Combined with the Pentagon's strike options, this looks like preparation for something bigger than a negotiated settlement.

Healthcare data of 3.4 million Americans just got exposed. Health tech company TriZetto confirmed hackers accessed systems as early as November 2024, stealing names, Social Security numbers, birth dates, addresses, and insurance details. If you have employer-provided health insurance in the US, your data may be in the wind.
